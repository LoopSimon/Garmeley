[[Gethricism|Gethric]] Clergy of Iztaw, the Head of the Gethrist Church which has canonized almost all Saints in History; Its Head is the High-Bishop, or in Vihri "Bisop", currently the blessed Orthel II.

Representing the oldest denomination of Gethricism, the Clerics and Priests of Iztaw are present in Chapels and Churches around the modern world.

What differentiates them from Clerics following [[Trinicism]] besides their actual teachings, is their organisation as the Iztaw Clergy follows a strict hierarchy of Priests, Clerics and Bishops that all report and answer to the office of the High-Bishop of Iztaw itself.

The vast funds and incredible influence on their worshippers have often led to this Institution of Faith to be seen as barely less than a Nation of its own, for many decades of history it was true that the High-Bishop was the most powerful man in [[Vihr]], not the King in Zavlech.

It is said that Agents of the Church take on forms and guises of all shapes and colours, and work from the light of the altar as much as from the shadows of an alleyway, but beyond anything else the Clergy values discretion and therefore their methods or involvement is usually left to speculations.

### Ranks within the Clergy:

 ##### High-Bisop of Iztaw

The High-Bisop of Iztaw is the Head of the Gethric Church and the foremost authority on all matters of canonical law and theological judgement, he is elected for life by all Bishops upon death, although not every Bishops vote is counted the same. The High-Bisop is also usually the Head of the political entity of [[Iztaw]] and thus formally a subject to the Ras of [[Vihr]].

 ##### Regional Archbishop / Primat / Patriarch

Certain Bishops are ordained as Archbishops and responsibly for an entire region, they usually are seen as more important and influential than the Bishops under them but certain exceptions for especially important Municipalities are sometimes made.
Archbishops are granted votes within the selection of the next High-Bisop, although their respective votes are weighed significantly more than that of the other Bishops.
A Primat is a workaround for the Church that still formally considers itself the head of its "lost" territories concerning the Gethric Schism, a Primat is essentially an Archbishop in the view of Iztaw that is not given any votes for the election of a new High-Bisop, but still acknowledged as a high-ranking servant of the Prophet. 
The Patriarchates of Kherova and Azuvke are given similar honorific status.

 ##### Bishop

A gethric Bishop is the overseer of an ecclesiastical province, they have oversight of their diocese and effective authority over the suffrage in their province, ensuring that the faith and ecclesiastical discipline are properly observed, that the authority of the Church is maintained and expanded and that the education within the lesser ranks of the clergy is prospering. Bishops are appointed to their provinces by the High-Bisop, though almost always under direct recommendation of the respective Arch-Bishop, they are granted a vote within the election of the High-Bisop.

 ##### Deacon

A Deacon is an ordained Minister or Father of the Faith that acts as a co worker and right-hand to the Bishop, but is usually focused on direct service, public outreach and more "direct" protection of the faith within the ecclesiastical province, a Bishop tends to have only a single Deacon although this tradition has been broken in the past, a Deacon is chosen at the personal request of his Bishop. In some cases the role of a Deacon is forgone entirely, particularly in Bishoprics that are smaller or work in close cooperation with a gethric Order of Knights or equivalent organization, when the Vicar simply assumes the remaining responsibility of the role.

 ##### Vicar

A vicar is an ordained Minister of Father of the Faith that assists the bishop in the material governance of the province, usually only one vicar is appointed but particularly large provinces may have more than on vicar. The Vicar tends to always be a local and more deeply engrained with regional politics, they are chosen by the regional Arch-Bishop and may keep their role upon a chance in Bishop, unlike a Deacon.

 ##### Father / Minister

A Father of the Church is a Priest that has finished his clerical education in the Minor Cudra and the Maior Cudra, as well as complete his Pilgrimage or Uwazna to Iztaw and be ordained by the High-Bisop. 
A Minister is a Priest that has also completed the Tregesia and is granted the right to practice clerical law.
Typically every major parish is headed by a Father or Minister, no female is granted this role, even upon finishing the aforementioned requirements as a Priest.

 ##### Priest
 
 A Priest is a member of clergy that has completed their Minor Cudra but not yet their Maior Cudra, the vast majority of Clerical officials are of this rank and take on most administrative and spiritual tasks. Many Priests are mentored by a Father or Minister.

 ##### Disciple

A Disciple is a person who has given themselves to the Church but not yet completed the first clerical exam, the Minor Cudra. Disciples are not allowed to take the Minor Cudra before the age of 15 and most dont complete it before the age of 22.

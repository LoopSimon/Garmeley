<p>In full the Königliche Gesellschaft für Westmeadischen Handel, meadi for "royal society of west-meadi trade" is a government chartered company unifying the old Guild Offices of Zeeport, Meadhavn and Dirdahl as well as the Département du Col as a single individual Company in the year 900PA, as a consequence of the Treaty of Lotsprings. The Gesellschaft was formed in the image of the successful and dominant <a href="Geldfield Unie van Vrijhandel" class="internal-link">Geldfield Unie van Vrijhandel</a> although is over 85% state owned. The crown has granted it, in accordance with the Treaty of Lotsprings, exclusive rights for trading in the western Sea and the colonization of islands found West of Sertova.</p>
<p>The Gesellschaft is lead by the Syndic, a permanent official appointed by the royal treasury advisory chair, to handle legal and diplomatic matters, as well as oversea individual diets. The first Syndic was Count Heinrich von Rendling, before the creation of the Company an important member of the von Fulmer regime under Gisela III. After the Purge of the so called "Fulmerites" by the Duke du Jera in his position as Prince Regent following Gisela IIIs death and the regency of his daughter <a href="Gisela IV" class="internal-link">Gisela IV</a>, von Redling was relieved of his position and Count Otto von Mird became the current Syndic. Ironically Gisela IV would reverse almost all of her fathers policies and restore von Fulmer to court, but it was decided to continue with von Mirds administration as Syndic in the interest of continuity in her early reign, as well as for the stability of the young Institution itself.</p>

<p>The Geldfield Unie van Vrijhandel, usually shortened to G.U.V. is one of the first joint stock companies in the world, modelled after principles of the international dwarven and gnomish cartels  and the example of internationally operating merchant conglomerates such as the <a href="Grand Ziflis Company" class="internal-link">elven Ziflis Company</a> as a greater union of many smaller existing trading companies, shipping Kontors and the old blijgrater merchants guilds. It is the first chartered trading company of its time created by independent citizens and was established in 861PA, even the GFZ that technically preceded it would later adopt many of its policies of autonomous trading cells. </p>
<p>The Company is headed by the Kamer van gelijke belangen, following the Charter of 891PA, the Chamber has elected an official representative, known as the Kamerspreker, currently this position is occupied by <a href="Pieter van Dielen">Pieter van Dielen</a> who has held it since the year 914PA.</p>
<p>The "Opening of the West" refers to the increasing trade volume between Sertovi and Othiri ports with the nations of the Torn Sea in the early 800s breaking the monopoly of the cevoli ports that had long had an iron grip on the merchants of Sertova and Othirdon as well as the contentious inland route through the Arvale Pass in the meadi Westerlands. Blijgrater Traders had more and more success in the ports of the West but had to continue to compete with the regional political interests of Nations such as Vignasoya, Baeling and the cevolian powers of the south, ultimately leading to a need of a unified body capable of negotiating lucrative commercial agreements with the various nations and individual ports of the region. </p>
<p>Over the course of the last 70 years the G.U.V saw an unprecedented growth and is largely responsibly for the commercial power of the League, however worries of the Company outgrowing the state itself or becoming too powerful as a free entity continued to amass, leading to what is now known as the Charter of 891. In 891PA, the G.U.V was forced by the Leagues Council of Cities, its governing body, to require at least 45% of its shared to be owned by the Chambers of the most important cities of the League, 15% respectively by Hevn, Schipwig and Geldfields, and those shares to be made untouchable by further diluting. With little choice left the Representatives of the Company oversaw large scale restructuring and the G.U.V had been irreversibly tied to the government of the League ever since.</p>
<p>As such, the League officially granted the Unie a 100 year monopoly to carry out all trade in Othirdon, Cevolia and Sertova, as well as oversee and overtake colonization efforts in the far away continent of <a href="Aisligos" class="internal-link">Aisligos</a>, that would grow continuously over the course of the 9th and 10th century. The Treaty of Lotsprings in 900PA was signed as a direct consequence of the Charter of 891 and would regulate oversea, colonization and trading interests between the Blijgrat League and <a href="Meadrath" class="internal-link">Meadrath</a> a decision that would lead to an unprecedented dominance over the Western Trade by the two countries. In this capacity, the Unie works surprisingly closely with the <a href="Gesellschaft für Westmeadischen Handel" class="internal-link">Gesellschaft für Westmeadischen Handel</a> that was largely created in the image of the G.U.V itself.</p>
<p>In many ways the Geldfield Unie van Vrijhandel became an example for not only other private businesses and larger trading companies founded in its wake, but also a sign of growing commercial forces and international diplomacy based on them for entire countries.</p>

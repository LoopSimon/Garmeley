<p>The Korsfall College of Astronomy and Magicks is one of the largest place of academic research and education into Arcane and Divine Magic, and home to the great <a href="Wulfrick" class="internal-link">Wulfrick</a>-Observatory.
The current Rector of Korsfall College is the Half-Elf Thomar of Whisperhill, it was founded in the year 556PA under the rule of King Gunther I von Kor. The College is an Institution of great renown and respect far beyond the courts of Meadland, Alumni are sometimes sent to the courts of nobility in advisory positions, primarily in the lands of Cevolia, Garmeley and, Urland. The Colleges reputation as a place of learning and scientific advancement in the realm of the arcane is only slightly tarnished by rumours of controversial practices, its extremely close ties to the state and active role in arcane policing and oversight over the people of Meadland. Ever since the reforms of <a href="Gisela II" class="internal-link">Gisela II</a> in the 8th century the influence and political responsibilities of the Institution have grown steadily, its given triple mandate is: Education, Advance and Control.
</p>
<p>The College works closely in cooperation with major western institutions of arcane and magical advancement and research, often informed and influenced by the political relationship between Meadrath and its neighbours, the most important organizations in eager exchange and partnership include The Hevn Academy of Planar Education, the Royal Academy of Battvia, the Peoples University of Albesan and the minor circles and magi towers in meadland, urland and Cevolia. Notable although a friendly rivalry exists between Hevn and Korsfall, the two institutions engage in healthy competition and have relied on each others resources in the past. <br> Furthermore the College enjoys active and exclusive partnerships with elements within the Excavators Guild of Butera and the Syndicate of Ott.</p>

<h3>The Ichori Choir</h3>
<p>The Ichori Choir is a department within the College that only answers to the Masters Council, an assembly of the four 5 major Chairs within the College, the Dean of the College and the courts envoy, the Master of the Ichori, themselves a major Chair of the College, is often seen as a figure more mysterious and influential than the Rectors Chair itself, and their responsibilities require a great level of freedom and secrecy. The Choir handles one of the three major columns of the Colleges Tasks: Control.</p>
<p>The first major role the Choir fulfils is that of acquisition and regulation of individuals capable of casting Magicks within the realm, including not only the <a href="Meadrath" class="internal-link">Crown</a> itself but also its more autonomous vassals such as <a href="Gisvale" class="internal-link">Gisvale</a>, <a href="Lesmere" class="internal-link">Lesmere</a>, <a href="Zmalig" class="internal-link">Zmalig</a> and <a href="Freelands" class="internal-link">the Freelands</a>. The college employs specialist agents, known as the Ichorhand that monitor and observe the urban and rural population as best they can, attempting to bring promising individuals, usually between the age of 5-11 to the College, immediately while also working as both a force to deter and fight of magical threats and rogue casters as best they can. The Ichorhand work with full authority of the College granted to it by the Royal Court of Korsfall, but is usually still rather hands off and apolitical, treading careful to not overstep their mandate of only regulating the occult and arcane. Inevitably this tasks brings with it a mixed reputation as while they often promise and give their children they take better lives and a bright future, as well as recompensating their families well, they in turn often have to make hard decisions and use force when necessary, leading to a moniker as "Child thieves" and the like.</p>
<p>The second major role of the Choir is the administration of the Ichor Binding itself: This enigmatic ritual is kept under extremely tight lock and key but was the essential innovation and promise of the Mages that would lead to their important role and political influence as well as great academic freedom following the reforms of the 8th century. Upon enrolling in a higher course, a learned mage, as well as strictly any and all trueborn applicant, is put under binding treaties of hemomatic nature that allow varying degrees of control and dominion above the Holder, this principle relationship between the College and its students is called the Ichorbond. </p>
<p>To be freed of the Ichorbond, whose observation, maintenance and usage is handled by the Choir, is a great privilege known as the Ichor Licta, traditionally only granted upon receiving certain honours and academic progress, for example becoming a Chair of the College itself, or after careful review and consideration upon an official Request.</p>

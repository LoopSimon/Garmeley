<p>This organization, often categorized as a gethric knightly order, was founded in the aftermath of the Conclave at Trinfurt in the 8th century and seeks to uphold trinicist values.</p>

<p>Named after the Iron Tower, the infamous castle in central Trinfurt at which foot local militia and heroes of the city fought of the attacking Knights of the <a href="Order of the Silver Wheel" class="internal-link">Silver Wheel</a>, this organization is decidedly apolitical and advocates for Peace in times of war, actively seeking out regions inflamed by wartime suffering to ease the life for innocent civilians and the everyman.</p>
<p>Unlike many militant or monastic orders that have become strict paramilitary organizations in the modern day, the Iron Tower is a less structured and military group that is often represented by equals of all kinds of callings, be it Knights and Paladins in shining armour or medicae and clerics in soothing robes. Traditionally one who seeks to join the ranks of the Iron Tower is judged by a Mentor, an experienced member of the Order, over the period of three years. The merit sought here is less that of magical prowess of physical ability, and more so that of the heart and soul. If an applicant is found worthy and swears to uphold the values of the Iron Tower they are welcomed into the ranks of the Order.</p>
<p>Owing to their decentralized structure and open minded attitude, the Iron Tower enjoys less favourable treatment by nobles, city keepers, administrators or Kings but is instead beloved by the men and women of lower standing who see them as true knights instead of feudal enforcers.</p>

<p>The patrons of the Iron Tower are the monks of the Abbey of the Even Touch, located north of Trinfurt and it is here where most applicants join the ranks of the Order. The current Grandmaster of the Iron Tower and spiritual leader of the organisation is Grandmaester Pater Lucien Cybel.</p>

<p>Izevkh is a Grachy city on the bend of the Jaerflow River, sitting beneath the Jaerfall Waterfall and opposite a small cluster of hills and mountains known as the Irdknuckles, it is a major hub of commerce within the more rural east and north of Grachy.</p>
<p>The City is home to the Church of Remembrance, also known as St <a href="Igor" class="internal-link">Igor's </a> Church but usually not called this due to the larger and more important church of the same name in the city of Modva, Izevkh Castle, and the Grey Mausoleum. The Jaerfall makes shipping from further up the river and the areas around Trozk impossible without coming through the land route into the city, similarly the many farmlands around Izevkh supply its northern neighbour Kharnov with valuable produce so the land routes coming in and out of Izevkh have made it a very popular destination for merchant caravans. The City profits from the political influence of its elected Leader, Gram Ruda, who's popular standing within the Traditionalist Faction of the Grand Ushkyn at Halgrach is very helpful in regards to expansionist charters granted by the central authority of the Republic. This has led to an extremely aggressive policy of deforestation and settlement along the northern Jaerflow, as Izevkhs  Boyars have begun to compete with the entrenched and rich Landowners of the West.</p>

<h2>Demographics</h2>
<p>The city is a majority grachy human settlement although humans from vihr and various bykmali traders make up small minorities. The largest minority however are Mistspire dwarves who enjoy great privilege in the city and are even given an honorary seat at the Ushykn of the Region for their contributions to Izevkhs growth and prosperity, many of them stem from the communities around <a href="Tizin-Kol " class="internal-link">Tizin-Kol </a> originally but are now a few generations removed from initial migrants.</p>
<h2>Government and Politics</h2>
<p>Like all regional Capitals in the Republic, Izevkh is governed by a Gram elected by the Ushkyn, currently a position held by Eril zi Ruda, an influential and powerful politician on the national stage, proud traditionalist and ambitious leader, aiming to build out the traditionally poorer backwater of eastern Grachy. The Ushkyn of the city convenes in the Orsrag Hall where zi Ruda holds court as well. </p>
<h2>Izevkh Casle & Riverview</h2>
<p>The Jaerstone Keep sits on the southern side of the Jaerflow across the Iron bridge, it was the residence of the zi Beriz Lines for houndreds of years until their Exile following the Boyars Rebellion of 571PA. It has been the Seat of the Gram in the days of the Grachy Republic and is currently the residence of the House of zi Ruda.</p>
<p>Riverview Tower, often called simply just Riverview is the cities prison and regarded as one of the safest dungeons in all of eastern Grachy, its Tower complex reaches many corridors high but also stretches underground and is well guarded by the City Watch. Many criminals from all over the Jaerflow meet their final days in Riverview.</p>
<h2>Church of Remembrance</h2>
<p>Officially "Saint Igors Church" but almost never referred to as such due to aforementioned reasons, the Church of Remembrance is nevertheless the largest religious building east of Halgrach and a main attraction for regional pilgrimages. Saint Igor, the Patron of Grachy, is worshipped here more than any other gethric Saint and the Church is said to hold many relics associated with Igor in its sacristy. Notably the Church complex is also home to the Nightingale Library, one of the largest archives of knowledge in the entirety of Nostroya, well kept by the Candleflame Guard. The Church is overseen by Bisop Dreywin, a younger member of the clergy that has risen within its ranks due to his sense for people and a critical sense for politics. The clergy of Izevkh keeps a good relationship with the Gram and supports the zi Rudas politics of local expansionism.</p>
<h2>Grey Mausoleum</h2>
<p>The Grey Mausoleum of Izevkh is a burial complex initially build in 469PA  by Ras Ivan II of Jerinov for members of his dynasty, but was later also used by members of the of Stir Dynasty that succeeded the of Jerinovs. The Grey Mausoleum lies south-west of the City walls in the Jaerhills north of the River. </p>
<h2>Dusttown & Igorsfield</h2>
<p>The areas south of the River are pressed between the rising mountains of the Jaerhills and the riverfront itself. Between Izevkh Castle and Riverview Prison lies Dusttown, a sprawling community making use of the space and largely concerned with the day to day mining operations near the city itsel, as the Jaerhills are home to a few mines like Lavek Mine and Bonemill Mine relatively close to the city.</p>
<p>The Igorsfield is an open space east of Dusttown surrounding the other side of Riverview, its north a small wood near the river and its central and larger part and open field used for military exercise, tournament, games and other attractions, as well as ceremonies and parades of worldly and religious nature.</p>
<p>The Three main bridges of Izevkh connect the southern parts to the central city, the oldest bridge is the Iron Bridge spanning from Izevkh Castle to the small island of Irwig and then the southern part of Izevkhs Oldtown. Jaerbridge in the centre of the city is the largest Bridge and build connecting Dusttown and Riverview with the city itself, when Izevkh kept prospering. Newbridge, aptly named, is the northernmost bridge and has only finished construction recently, mainly sponsored by various guilds and local businesses that aim to connect the remaining available space south of the river to the centre of the city.</p>
<h2>Oldmarket and Zarsroad</h2>
<p>The Zarsroad cuts through the entire City and is the central avenue for all traffic within Izevkh, flowing from the Springsgate in the west to the Tergate in the east. In the centre of Zarsroad, connecting it northwards to the Greengate and southwards to the Jaerbridge and Dusttown lies the Oldmarket. Oldmarket is a large square within Izevkh and home to the daily market of goods from out and inside the city, as well as various important commercial and administrative buildings such as the Orsrag Hall where the Ushkyn meets, the famous Lekeib & Zerek House of Commerce and the Kalny Exchange.</p>
<p>The Zarsroad leaves the city westwards and continues on to Melesk and later the Capital City Halgrach going further alongside the Halgrior River flowing through southern Grachy by the cities of Bryansk, Modva and Ustikiv finally ending in the Port City of Voblag itself. It is the most important commercial lane of land travel in the entire Republic.</p>
<h2>West Izevkh</h2>
<p>The western part of the City is home to its second largest Church, Everwarm, many commercial buildings and smaller markets, the cities Treasury, various reputably Inns and  larger residential areas. A large portion of the cities dwarven population lives here and the Ushykn of the city has even granted their community the right to construct a Temple to their Terem-Zal Gods.</p>
<h2>Northside</h2>
<p>The wealthiest area of the City, Northside is home to the Villas, Estates and Cottages of various Boyars and the like, as well as the tallest building in the city, Borni Tower. Constructed by the Astronomer Gregory Borni in the 9th century, the Tower is home to an impressive Observatorium and a mark of pride a city this far east, a region considered uneducated and poor.</p>
<h2>The Rags</h2>
<p>The Southeastern Quarter of Izevkh, often simply referred to as "the Rags" is the poorest part of the city, hounded by criminals and prone to illegal activities, the Jaerkontor in the south is the most well guarded part of the entire River and the main entry point for ships from the smaller communities upriver.</p>

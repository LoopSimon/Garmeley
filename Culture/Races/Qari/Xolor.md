<p>Vaguely related to the <a href="Orc" class="internal-link">Qari</a> peoples, the Xohor are a species native to far-eastern Nirydia, they are the predominant inhabitants of the Terenni Isles and similar to the Orcs have a longstanding tradition of seafaring. </p>
<p>Their origin is unknown but it is often speculated that they hail from the home of the Orcs, the island of Qa as well but migrated east and at some point underwent a change that caused their mutations and differences. The skin of the Xolor is almost always of a grey to blue-ish shade, their bodies producing a unique protein that makes them easily amass fat in a protective layer around their muscles. This also causes many of them to appear bloated or obese when compared to a human or orc.</p>
<p>Xolor are able to store organic memories within this protein that can be understood and absorbed by them when eaten, leading to a culture of ritual cannibalism in traditional Xolor society. Food culture and cuisine has a revered place within their communities and belief systems but this element of Xolor physiology and cultural practices is also a leading reason for why they tend to be ostracized by other cultures and communities.</p> <p>Xolori memory eating can lead to full on characteristics of the consumed to be obtained, from actual memories to physical traits and even resulting mutations. In the most severe cases, when an individual consumed a large amount of memories this way, they become what is referred to as a Chimera: a Xolor being that can only sustain themselves on the flesh of creatures that are capable of higher thought, be it Xolor, Orc or most other sentient creatures. Such a creature often fully mutates and changes appearance, shape and personality.</p>
<p>During the <a href="Zuhurism" class="internal-link">Zuhuri</a> Purges of the 8th and 9th century a great number of Xolor were exterminated as the species was declared a "shadow on Pen-Zuhurs Visage". To date the tales of horrifying treatment of the Xolor once populous on the Nirydian mainland are not fully revealed to the public.</p>

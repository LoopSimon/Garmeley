Numerous and ingenious in their variance, humans are by far the most popular race in Vihr and Ilrakhi, they tend to organize in large communities and hierarchies, flocking to religion, magic and charismatic leaders who can promise salvation in this life or the next.

Humans tend to live up to 80 years naturally, although spells or the mere prolonged exposure to magic are known to extend this while many can also fall under this expectancy due to regional infrastructure and wealth. 

Humanity has produced many legends and heroes, founded Faiths and forged arcane wonders, while their greatest strength is adaptability, they also tend to excel less in niche aspects of life, warfare or academia.  
Nevertheless it is precisely in this incredible diversity of thought and body that humanity has found the strength to bend most of the known world to its will.

Human cultural groups on Ilrakhan, Behera and Nirydia include:

Bykmali: primarily the Olbellinge, Jueli and Skori people of Trykkerog.

Cevoli: primarily the Cesteri, Battvian and Rasteri peoples

Meadi: including High Meadi, Low Meadi, Gismen, and Hundi peoples

Weadi: primarily the Weadmer, Kranmer, Gerdien and Greenfolk peoples

Urien: primarily Eldien and Trinmen peoples

Reani: primarily Edgrali, 

Garmian: primarily the High Garmian, Lower Garmian and Melmen  

Pilvian: primarily the Grevian and Fredna peoples

Asefi: primarily the Horadi, Hessefi and Asefi peoples

Beheri: primarily the Beheri, Arevi, and Mogori tribes

Palmunian: including the remnant tribes of Neo-Palmunis peoples

Vihrmen: primarily the Trydzi, Opuzni, Tloky and Trazci peoples

Grachy: primarily the Halgrachi, Hyllfolk and Giormen peoples

Wermian: including the Old Wermian, Moormen and Morellian people

Kheri: primarily the Lakiri, Azuvi, Kheri and Yevani peoples

Beali: primarily the Blue Beali, Red Beali and Golden Beali peoples

Othiri: primarily the Othiri, Lissari and Moreni peoples

The Gurita are a race of sentient mollusks, closely resembling Octopoda.
They are bilaterally symmetric, with two eyes, three hearts and a beak in the centre of their eight limbs, often simply referred to as tentacles. Unlike octopodes they simultaneously have a gill and a lung as respiratory structures, which are represented within their mantle cavity, allowing them to be amphibious in natures, they are gonochoristic, meaning that each individual is either male or female.
At the largest they can grow up to a weight of 60 kg and a radial span of up to 6 metres.

A gurita individual goes through several cycles over its lifespan during which they gradually replace almost all cellular structure, including and not limited to brain cells.
This cyclic replenishment of organic matter allows them to regrow almost all parts of their body, often martial injuries lead to either immediate death or none at all as long as the core abilities of nourishment are sustained, a gurita can theoretically even "survive" the destruction of their brain.
While this allows any gurita to essentially live forever, after the fulfilment of a given cycle the gurita enters into a stage called Memnesence, during which essential brain functions revert to that of a newborn, almost all memories of past cycles is lost upon completion of Memnesence.
A period between Memnecesne-cycles in the lifespan of a Gurita, colloquially just referred to as "a Cycle" tends to last between 20 and 30 years.

Gurid eggs have large yolks and tend to be greater in size and fewer in number than regular ocotpoda, however even with their birth-rates tending to be lower, their nature as biologically immortal beings is the cause for most societal evolution within the species, as overpopulation is the largest issue faced by most Gurid communities.

Unlike normal ocotopodea, they have evolved to no longer utilize ink-bags due to their amphibious nature, but still possess the remnants of the organ.
They possess the ability to perceive sound but can only view their surroundings in shades of grey, which at the same time gives them exceptional vision in darkness.
With the help of their eight legs it is possible for them to jet underwater and even propel themselves a short distance out of water.

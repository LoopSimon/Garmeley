<p>The Term Fey is a general catch-all term describing all manner of creatures present within the spiritual and material realm at the same time. Within certain traditions they are worshiped or feared as supernatural guides or antagonists, sometimes even referred to as Gods. Due to their nature they often act as emissaries from the spiritual to the material realm but are not bound to the same forces spiritual beings such as the Greater Spirits are, often showing much more mortal traits and clear personalities. Some regions of the World seem to be more or less prone to Fey but in reality this is likely owed to the environments preferred by the Fey to materialize in, as they often show a disdain to civilization building and urban or artificial environments. Fey often influence and manipulate mortals, especially so with the so called "Greater Fey" that have been worshiped in cults and religions throughout history and often get mistaken as spirits.</p>
<h4>Known Greater Fey</h4>
</h6><a href="Lempo" class="internal-link">Lempo</a></h6>
<p>The "Father of Moths" Lempo is a Fey that has appeared in Nostroya and Trykkerog.</p>
</h6><a href="Bar-Dilian" class="internal-link">Bar-Dilian</a></h6>
<p>A Fey that is rumoured to have materialized in Vihr, Trykkerog, Nostroya and even Urland.</p>
</h6><a href="Loomman" class="internal-link">The Loomman</a></h6>
<p>Primarily associated with Trykkerog and Nostroya.</p>
</h6><a href="Parniva" class="internal-link">Parniva</a></h6>
<p>Parniva is "native" to the lands of the Stormy Sea and old Rean, having been seen in modern day Hainach, Garmeley & Grevenna.</p>

</h6><a href="Plysfir" class="internal-link">Plysfir</a></h6>
<p>With a preference to similar regions, Plysfir is the immortal rival of Parniva.</p>

</h6><a href="Yari" class="internal-link">Yari</a> as Fey</h6>
<p>A common theory is that the Beheran Yari, supernatural and immortal beings often worshiped as gods, are in reality Fey. Although there are no reports of them simply vanishing or disappearing into the spiritual realm. Some Yari are also the Centrepoint's for entire civilizations and political structures, residing within the apex of massive cities, which also goes against the argument for their fey nature.</p>

</h6>Religious Conflations & Spirits</h6>
<p>Within every greater Cult of Spirit Worship, be it Garmian & Reani Worship of the <a href="Kindly Lords" class="internal-link">Kindly Lords</a>, the Gods of the <a href="Aeldcult" class="internal-link">Aeldcult</a>, Trykkerogi  <a href="Bykmali Paganism" class="internal-link">Spiritworship</a> or the hundreds of other cults and belief systems based on these entities there are often theories of some if not all of the worshiped beings being actually Fey, specifically when reports of a gods actual materialization occur, one should careful consider if they are dealing with a spiritual force of nature or maybe with a Fey with their own agenda.</p>
<h2>Hags</h2>
<p>A Hag is a supernatural creature, that results of the Ascendence within a Circle of shamanistic practitioners. If successful a member of such a Circle may absorb the physical and spiritual energy of his fellows and ascend or "descend" into hagdom retaining some of their own physical characteristics but also obtaining some of their victims.</p>
<p>It is important to note that Circles tend to be surprised and betrayed by the Hags actions and usually are originally formed as peaceful and communal groupings to help each other understand their spiritual and magical capabilities.</p>
<p>Hags are incredibly in tune with both the physical and material realm, walking freely between them but being bound to their location within both, often times this results in a Hags permanent residence becoming something of a lair for the creature.</p>

<h2>Aktomi</h2>
<p>A spider-like spirit who lives as a hermit in the wilderness. Cunning and selfish, he is always looking for opportunities to trick travellers, steal their possessions, or manipulate others for his own gain. Many stories portray them as more of a nuisance than a true monster, but those who underestimate an Aktmoi often find themselves losing far more than they expected.</p>

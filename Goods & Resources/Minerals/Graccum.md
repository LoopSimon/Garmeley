<p>Graccum, also known as wealdstone or godsbone, is a silvery-white element, hard and corrosion-resistant, it was named by Voga Birena, a famous grachy alchemist of the 6th century after its alchemical and magical properties were first classified and discovered by her. Graccum had been used by the native people of <a href="Nostroya" class="internal-link">Nostroya</a> for likely over a thousand years prior to her discovery in religious architecture. It is extremly rare to be found and one of the most valuable precious metals. Graccum ores are a mixture of other metals such as iron and the separation of it from iron is a dangerous and usually toxic process conducted in degrees varying in efficiency and safety.</p>
<p>Graccum is desired by alchemists for its unique kynotic effect; when used the object of Infusion during classical <a href="Alchemy" class="internal-link">Kynosis</a>, it loses essentially zero planar energy resulting in an incredibly efficient Kynogma. Graccum based alchemical constructs are extremely coveted and well traded, they can act as a sort of "supercharger" for prisms in <a href="Artificery" class="internal-link">the practice of Prosum</a> and are sometimes said to even ease the access to planar energy for organic casters.</p>
<p>Weapons made of Graccum are rare and considered great treasures, often the basis for advanced magical artefacts and other objects.</p>
<p>The largest commercial Graccum Mine in the known world is near Solny in southern <a href="Grachy" class="internal-link">Grachy</a>.</p>

Dainosticism, sometimes referred to as Insabaorism, is a philosophy that describes the worship of the Dainos, the Spark to exist itself, the hidden truth that must exist to explain the search for itself.

Dainosticism is a response to the thought experiment of the Sabaor, both extremely obscure within contemporary culture.
A Sabaor describes a projection of ones consciousness itself, capable of manipulating the waking mind, one can - on an elemental level - consider any creature, environment, fact or thought itself a Sabaor. 
Naturally such thinking quickly leads one down a rather absolute view of existence, if anything one considers can be a Sabaor, and at the core of a Sabaors nature lies its nature as an intrinsic part of oneself, then there is not much left to consider other than the motive behind any given Sabaor itself.


Classic Dainosticism asks, accepting the Sabaor as conceptually un-disprovable reality, what remains but the search for motive, for that is the nature of the very philosophy.
If the "Great Sabaor", meaning the total denial of anything originating from outsides ones mind, truly exists, then what is motive behind his precise nature as ones specific perceived reality.
In other words, if reality is self made fiction, then why make specifically this reality?
The answer to this question is referred to as the Dainos, and worshipped by the few true adherents to this philosophy that do not succumb to madness or suicidal tendencies in almost religious fashion.


The temporal conclusion, explaining that we do not exist within time but rather as a single frozen moment in consciousness, is an obscure school within Dainosticism:
According to its followers, one only exists in a single moment as the determined effect of all information, perceived as memories, lacking any concrete proof for the past to have ever happened or the future to ever occur. 
In this view, we are no more than single assets of data, alike to a number representing a single value in a greater construct of incomprehensible scale and nature.

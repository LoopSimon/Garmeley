<p>Doggerism is an ancient and organised Faith that worships a Pantheon of four deities, the gods Lorel, Vanter, Wissger and Killag. It was the largest and dominant faith in ancient  <a href="Geol" class="internal-link">Geol</a>, as well as the Kingdom of  <a href="Spursley" class="internal-link">Spursley</a> and its successor, the undead state of  <a href="Bilsworth" class="internal-link">Bilsworth</a>.</p>

<p>The Faith is based and named after the teachings and visions of Aelfred Dogger, whos journey through the lands of the western Beheran coast is documented in the Doggerad, the holy book of the faith. While a mortal himself, Aelfred is sometimes considered an elevated figure within the more religious aspects of his religion, ranging from a simple prophet to an almost demigod like avatar of all four divine Aspects. Aelfred lived and wrote during the 2nd century PA.</p>

<p>The four deific aspects are based on the Seasons of Spring, Summer, Autumn and Winter and tend to take on oppressive or threatening roles within cultures. The Dogger Gods offer power, riches, happiness and answers but always with the background of a fear of death, ruin and destruction ever looming.</p>

<p>Lorel</p>
<p>The Mother of Monsters, Lorel is usually depicted as a ocean-born female controlling the nefarious kraken of the Tirean Ocean, she represents the Season of Spring, the birth of man and destiny.</p>
<p>Vanter</p>
<p>The Red Devil, Vanter is usualy depicted as a red-skinned devil that can take over the bodies and forms of anyone he desires, he represents the Season of Summer, the Power of man and destruction.</p>

<p>Wissger</p>
<p>The Broker, Wissger is usually depicted as a massive grey horned demon offering deals and promises to worshippers and victims, he represents the Season of Autumn, the Desires of man and dreams.</p>
<p>Kallag</p>
<p>The Lord of Bones, Kallag, is usually depicted as a Skeletal Figure, sometimes armed as he offers answers and resolution, he represents the Season of Winter, the Fall of man and Meaning.</p>

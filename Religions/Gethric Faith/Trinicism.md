<p>The newest and least organized denomination was founded  following the Conclave at Trinfurt and the Gethric Schism in 764PA. Largely a response of Western Bishops in Cevolia and Meadland to the extreme authority and control of the <a href="Church of Iztaw" class="internal-link">Iztaw</a> Clergy and the start of its anti-elven Pogroms, it acted as a valve for the emerging western realms to take control away from what they perceived to be a Vihri 
state-controlled entity and allow the citizens of their own countries to attempt to achieve the afterlife in their own way.</p>  
<p>With only regional Authorities and a much more progressive outlook, Trinicism may have produced less miracle workers and divine leaders, but if often heralded as a Faith for truly everyone, that celebrates merit and equality over right of birth.
Vows here often include attempts at change and reflection of one or one's environment, ambitions and dreams.</p>

<small><i>The Conclave at Trinfurt</i></small>

<p>Traditionally, Trinicists hold the Scriptures of the White Book to be the only divinely inspired text, the only presently available source of divinely revealed knowledge, and the only infallible source of Gethrics doctrine. The role of a High-Bisop to represent the Prophet in our plane is nonsensical to most modern Trinicists, to emulate the Saints and follow the Prophets teachings, the first step has to be to acknowledge their humanity and the strength and conviction they wrought from it. Within Trinicist doctrine Gethric was a mere man enlightened through circumstance and character, not a divine hero ordained at birth and realized at opportunity. The highest office within the Trinicist Church is that of the Bishop, with no clear hierarchy between individual Bishops or greater regional authority, although the old offices of the Archbishoprics of Cevolia, Meadland, Weadland and so forth are still held by the Bishop of Jiedeux, Bellhome and Schipwig respectively they hold no effective dominion over other Bishops within their titular dominion. <p>
</p>The cause of Trinfurt was actively supported by many worldly rulers, such as Queen <a href="Gisela II" class="internal-link">Gisella the Good</a> of Meadrath at the time, that saw the clergy as a threat to their hegemony. The effects of this can clearly be seen in the difference between a Vihri or Uri ecclesiarchical province and a trinicist Bishopric: No religious tithe is raised by trinicist Bishops, instead they finance themselves through donations and worldly business, no clerical militia, the Candleguard of the old Church, is allowed to be raised independently by a Bishop, and legal authority is generally restricted to only cases on church grounds.</p>

<p>The role of women within the clergy has not changed much in comparison to the restricted possibilities of women within the old Church, as women are still not permitted to rise high within the individual clerical dominions, something that might be challenged with future missionary work within Othirdon, a region classically more egalitarian that has seen matriarchically structures in society many times across its history.</p>

<p>Trinicists accept most Saints with the notable exception of <a href="Andre" class="internal-link">Andre of Trydlew</a>, who is seen as a symbol of Iztaws corruption and false convictions. <a href="Markur" class="internal-link">Saint Markur</a> and <a href="Yahrwick" class="internal-link">Saint Yahrwick</a> are likely the most popular Saints within the overall Trinicist community if such a statement can even be made.</p>

<h2>Regional Trincisim outside of Cevolia, Meadland and Weadland</h2>
<p>Urien Trinicism is a question of political and clerical discourse, while the majority of the Ur region practices old Gethricism, a significant portion of the populations within Uhl, Trinvale and Gelmsburg prefer trinicist rites and actively reject the authority of gethric Bishops.</p>

<p>The Conversion and Missionary work within Othirdon has been steadily spreading, encouraged by many Meadi Monarchs that have sought to "open" the western Lands and seen religious conversion as a first step to integration and cultural and economic conquest. The largest Trinicist community within Othirdon is located within the eastern Lissvalley and the City of Varlys, where a trinicist Bishop resides in his own church.</p>

<p>Attempts at converting the residents of western Trykkerog, the citizens of ancient Vignasoya, have been largely unresponsive, as a large part of their modern cultural traditions and contemporary history is already defined on the countries resistance to the Gethric Conversions of Melmer that  saw most of the bykmali people exchange their old gods for the Gethric Flame.</p>

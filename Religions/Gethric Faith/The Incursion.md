<p>Sometimes referred to as the Wermian Incursion and often split in the first and second Incursion, this event describes the apocalyptic Fall of the old Kingdom of <a href="Wermia" class="internal-link">Wermia</a> in the early 3rd century PA and the subsequent foundation of the Gethric Faith. It is widely considered to be one of the most important events in the history of Inara, given the widespread and long dominance of <a href="Gethricism" class="internal-link">Gethricism</a> and the subsequent Wermian Accords that both continue to influence contemporary life. </p>

<p>A great deal of information in regards to the lead up to the 1st Incursion, the exact ongoings during the event and the immediate fallout are simply not reliably documented or more likely under clerical secrecy given the subject matter. </p>
<h2>Lead up</h2>
<p>The Kingdom of Wermia at the start of the 3rd century was a rich and powerful nation of early feudal society, with a strong magical culture and connection between its <a href="Turvik Paganism" class="internal-link">Turvik</a> clergy and thriving arcane estate. It can be roughly viewed as part of the ancient <a href="Augurism" class="internal-link">auguric</a> tradition as far as magical development is concerned, many rulers and influential personalities in later Wermia were magically gifted, with many historians suggesting unnatural bargains for planar power being struck. </p>
 <p> Wermia as a Region in todays mind is defined as the lands east of the Karmon River and west of the Oathhills, but the Kingdom in its height stretched out further, often clashing with the old Kingdom of <a href="Urland" class="internal-link">Urland</a> and the various settled tribes and petty Kings of the Vihri. Following the union of the greatest of the Vihri tribes inside of the <a href="Vihrmen Federation" class="internal-link">Vihrmen Federation</a> in approximately the year 130PA, Wermia saw itself continuously losing ground in her wars with the Horn Lords of Vihr, reaching a devastating point with the siege of Tawring, modern day <a href="Iztaw" class="internal-link">Iztaw</a>, in 202PA. At its Height Wermia had controlled lands as far east as the Tryd but was now struggling to keep control of her dominions at the Oathhills if even that.</p>
 <h2>The Crimson Rubric</h2>
 <p>The Crimson Rubric, sometimes simply referred to as "The Rubric" or the "Wermian Gate" is the name given to the fateful spell woven by the Crimson Circle, Wermias most powerful magi. Any considerations for historical accuracy and detailed retellings are obliged to rescind their words when it comes to details of magical incantations, according to the Wermian Accords. Suffice it to say that the Rubric created what is now known as a permanent Fluxbridge in Werm, the Capital of Wermia and was officially declared complete only in 240PA.</p>
 <p>Political considerations were likely the deciding factor for the Rubric, but historians agree that the conditions for such an event were ready and had been for a long time, in Wermia sure, but if not for its tragic example and apocalyptic consequence likely elsewhere sooner or later. The dualistic nature of the Incursion, first at Werm and later at Tawring, could give insight in the exact Argument, Petition and Invocation of the Rubric, but no soul foolish enough to ask such questions shall find detailed answers here. </p>

<h2>The First Incursion</h2>
 <p>In April of 215PA the Greenwood, Wermias central Forest surrounding its capital, broke apart. After what is likely to have been the completion of the Incantation Step during the Rubric the entire Greenwood burst aflame in a gigantic planar release of Energy, thousands of people likely died during the first hour of the event but little is known of the exact consequences. The Ravenhold, Werms royal castle was plucked apart and suspended within an extension of the Abyss itself from one moment to the next: Its stones changed to water, to wind, to roiling fire and back to stones within seconds; people were seen becoming babes and elders, their bones skin and their skin blood; a great pillar of colour arose from the castle and took away what gave things depth and age. </p>
 <p>How the effects of the first incursion were contained so quick to only Ravenhold itself is an eternal mystery to us but a great host of <a href="Daemon" class="internal-link">Daemons</a> had managed to manifest before hand and reach the other side of the Castle Walls. The rule of Chaos, slaughter and change of thousands, defilement and rape of land and people, that followed would last for almost 4 years, until essentially the entire capital city, the majority of the wermian population and the landscape of central Wermia had been changed, killed and sacrificed by the Crimson Circle. In 219, an Urien scout reported a sudden change in the abyssal land as almost all of wermias surviving residents had flown to either its coastal and somewhat safer communities or across the western Moor and the eastern Hills towards anything but daemonic Terror. The report described a rainbow like vortex of coloured clouds somewhere above the area of the former Capital, and soon after the disappearance of the shadows from beyond that had come to visit our Plane.</p>
 <p>The First Incursion would result in the end of Wermia as a nation, the destruction of almost all its major cities, the death of around 92% of its population, and the permanent defilement of the Greenwood, after only known as the Garden of Horrors, only the Ebon Tower, seat of the Crimson Circle itself stood pristine and unharmed. The worst however, was yet to come.</p>
 <h2>The Second Incursion</h2>
 <p>Over six years after the End of the first Incursion, in the summer of 226PA, the events of the second Incursion would begin as Holweid, a former wermian now vihri town north of the Oathhills became a Gate to the Abyss. Survivors of the even describe a giant mirror like disc of water forming in oval shape above the city, growing every minute only to spiral in on itself into an infinity of colours, like an inverse Tornado that seemed to define physics and comprehension. It should be noted that of the few eye witnesses that survived Holweid, all committed suicide in the same year.</p>
 <p>Where the first Incursion seemed to have been a devastating and nightmarish but regional and almost controlled catastrophe of sacrifice and betrayal, the Legions of Daemons that entered real space at Holweid acted more like a wave of destruction seeking to conquer and consume all life and land. Houndreds of Monsters in shapes and forms indescribable marched west and north from Holweird, quickly warping, changing and destroying all life in their way. This Host of Daemons encountered real resistance for the first time in 227PA at the fortified Walls of Tawring.</p>

 <p>Tawring held for almost 4 months before its central Keep was laid to ground, all the while a great coalition of men and dwarfs assembled in Vihr and Urland. But the first spark of hope in the hearts of the brave came with the news that a young <a href="Gethric" class="internal-link">man</a>, a former farmer from the disputed wermian territories, had managed to defeat a Daemon of the Abyss with strange powers, a miracle of white flames and terrible power abjured the creature from beyond, the first miracle of the Prophet.</p>
 <p>The farmer, a man called Gezric, later known as the prophet Gethric, surrounded himself with a group of righteous heroes and led as many survivors as possible out of Tawring before the city fell, among them men and women of all credes of life: farmers, lords and sages. Gethric would become the leader of the Vihri Union of Vanquishers as the assembled forces of the Federations warriors called themselves. Soon discoveries were made: Steel tempered in the supernatural powers of Gethric and his companions and handled with the expertise of old wermian blacksmiths was able to hurt the invaders, otherwise invincible to iron and bronze.</p>
 <p>The roots of Gethricism as a Faith would begin then, when the Prophet led his armies against the forces of the Abyss, his legendary meeting with <a href="Volboris" class="internal-link">Volboris</a> who could eradicate disease and heal the most terrible wounds with a glance, his fateful encounter with <a href="Razmyr" class="internal-link">Razmyr</a> who could see the events of past and future, the duel at Garzo Hill with the Lidless Fiend, the arrival of the Urien Fleet at Trazlov and Gethrics Speech at the Lehva Bank would all be recorded and retold first as mere inspiration but soon as divine belief amongst the brave men and women of his days.</p>
 <p>The Daemonic Host and Gethrics War against it would last for 14 years, too many zealous defenders of our world perished, and the abyssal forces advanced as far east as modern day Szievek and as far south as Lake Zav. The Cult of the few would grow into the belief of the many, the righteous flames of Gethric would find candles to light in every family, in every home and castle and many devout warriors would find themselves embraced by its warmth, given powerful abilities and invincible hope of their own.</p>

 <p>The Great Daemon whom the Prophet cursed Vazmoris, Well of Despair, was slain at Tawring by Gethric himself when the forces of our world reclaimed the lost city, dubbing it Iztaw and vowing to rebuild its walls again. Gethric would end the Rubric and abjure the Daemons of the Abyss in the Fall of 240PA, it is said that as the man who was a farmer and became a Prophet burned in the white light of hope, so did every vile creature summoned by the wermian Magi with him, no trace was left of the man that had saved our plane, but his words and lessons were recorded by Razmyr and his Followers united and led by Volboris, thus was the Faith founded and the Incursion ended.</p>
 <p>In the immediate Aftermath of the Incursions, during the early Vihri Wars of Unification, scouts and adventurers were send on cautious missions deeper and deeper inside the devastated landscape of Wermia, many never to return and many describing horrors of daemonic nature, living and unliving haunting the land still. The Ebon Tower, Seat of the Crimson Circle and Axis of the Rubric had simply vanished, not to be found wether intact or in ruins.</p>
 <h2>The Foundation of the Church</h2>
<p>The Iztaw Three, Gethric, Volboris and Razmyr were only two and as Razmyr started concerning himself more and more with Theology, his great work on the White Book and the matter of the soul, it fell to Volboris to organize the community of worshippers that had not only formed around him, but quickly spread like a wildfire through Urland and Vihr. Volboris would lay the foundations of the Clergy and is officially seen as the first High-Bisop of Iztaw, where the new church would make her home.</p>

Ilonism, also known as "The Cult of the Wheel" is a polytheistic Faith, often ascribed as a philosophy, that worships the so called "divine Wheel", the three goods that make up the spans of this wheel are Agmor, Welmo and Ruxis, representing the past, the present and the future.
The Wheel is generally understand in the ilonist sense as a process of transformation of the world around oneself through oneself first, seen best within its first tenant.
"Ilon" refers to the beheran word for truth, usually represented as the centre of the wheel that the three divine gods spin on as its spokes.
Ilonism is treated as a distinct tradition with its own scriptures and ritual systems but has long been closely connected with beheren folk religions, spirit worship and elemental cults.

Ilonism is practiced throughout northern Behera, in various local expressions be it but almost always maintaining its core tenants. The humans inhabiting the Desert and its various waterside population centres have held to the Wheel for thousands of years, primarily the Asefi, Subnimbi and Yorgosi peoples.
The dominance of the [[Elbae]] Sultanate in the region has changed how certain cultures practice the faith, some revolutionary elements in regions like Tusul engaging in a much more warlike interpretation of the Wheel, although the elves of the desert almost entirely maintain a policy of religious freedom in regards to the sultanate itself and its neighbouring polities.

The core tenants of Ilonism are:

To change the world one must first change himself.

The Wheel turns always, we are merely water on its spokes.

Ilon is unknowable, the unnameable mystery of the world that can never be taught.

He who fights Agmor, Welmo and Ruxis fights himself.

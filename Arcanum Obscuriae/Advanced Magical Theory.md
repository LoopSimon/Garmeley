<h2>Chapter 3: Spellcraft</h2>
<h4>The Auguri Invocation</h4>
<p>The Korsfall tradition taught here would like to emphasize the modern nature of the division between what a layman may call "Divine" and "Arcane" Magic and the how our historiographical approach can cause consideration thereof. Many times in the history of the known world, which extensively is the history of Magic, spiritual tradition, religion, cultural rite and academic magic are not only closely related but undividable, a fact that is immediately underlined by our first subject, the Auguri of ancient Meadland.</p>
<p>The classic Auguri invocation, as described on the Stone of Burd, follows a simple three step formula that goes as follows:</p>
<p>The Invocation:</p>
<p>The Augur, using the specific title and epithet of the desired aspect, invokes Ralfantis to channel their wisdom.</p>
<p>The Argument:</p>
<p>The Augur, reminding the Aspect of favours, deeds, gains and promises, offers argument to Ralfantis, so that they may sculpt the world according to their imagination.</p>
<p>The Petition:</p>
<p>The Augur, describing vividly the future they envision offering constraints and limitations, wish to Ralfantis to make true their desires.</p>
<p>The Dialogue-esque structure of the Auguri Invocation is similar to the ancient <a href="Turvik Paganism" class="internal-link">Turvik</a> prayers, often paired with sacrifice and offering, but viewed from the lense of our more advanced understanding of energy manipulation speaks to a remarkable, if not entirely self aware, understanding of key aspects every caster is painfully aware of even now: Precise thought, clean restraint and preservation of the Ego.</p>

<p>In <a href="Nostroya" class="internal-link">nostroyan</a> folklore and arcane taxonomy, a Sinlapsi (sometimes referred to as a Nostroyan Wight or Mother’s Vengeance) is a highly dangerous, malevolent undead entity born from profound grief, betrayal, and dark magic. Materializing as the physical manifestation of a mother's dying curse or absolute despair, the Sinlapsi is an entity of pure vengeance tethered to the natural world.</p>
<p>Origins and Etiology</p>
<p>The manifestation of a Sinlapsi is an exceptionally rare phenomenon within Nostroya. Arcane scholars and <a href="Aeldcult" class="internal-link">Aeld</a> druids believe that the entity cannot be summoned through traditional necromancy; rather, it requires a specific, tragic convergence of emotional agony and betrayal.

The curse is most commonly triggered when a newborn child is murdered, particularly in acts of domestic violence, such as by the hands of a cheated or enraged father. The sheer psychic weight of the mother's anguish and her resulting curse interacts with the ambient magic of the land. This localized despair tears a vengeful spirit from the ether, binding the mother's wrath to the surrounding flora and fauna to exact her revenge.</p>
<p>Witness accounts of a Sinlapsi describe a terrifying amalgamation of death and vibrant nature. The entity possesses impossibly long, gaunt, and skeletal pale limbs, and is typically shrouded in deep crimson cloth.
Its most striking feature is its head and shoulders, which are enveloped in a grotesque, crown like mane of vibrant summer flowers, blooming daisies, and dried brush. Its face is pale and hollow, possessing sunken, lidless eyes that glow with a baleful yellow-orange light.</p>
<p>Unlike traditional wights or undead that bring the chill of the grave, a Sinlapsi's arrival violently disrupts the local climate. Because the Sinlapsi is a creature of blind, localized vengeance, it generally does not wander far from the site of the tragedy that spawned it, aggressively attacking anyone who steps foot on its cursed grounds.</p>

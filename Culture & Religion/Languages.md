Languages of the World


  

Vihri Languages (can all reasonable understand each other somewhat)

Vihri

National Language of Vihr and the clerical language of Gethricism

Grachi

National Language of Grachy

Wermian

Spoken still in various Wermian villages in modern day Vihr

  

Urien Languages (can all reasonable understand each other somewhat)

Old Urien

Now mostly extinct, old Urien was the language of the unified Urien tribes in the early centuries PA when Urland existed.

These various dialects are spoken in the Petty Kingdoms.

Urien

The closest to a “Lingua Franca” on the Continent, Urien is the national language of Garmeley and the various petty Kingdoms, and spoken in almost all major cities of the continent.

  

Reani

The language of the Reani tribes that is now mostly spoken in Hainach, Grevenna and eastern Garmeley.

Lower Urien

Not just a dialect but now a distinct tongue, the language spoken in Uhl and north of Gelmsburg.

  

Elvish Languages (can all reasonable understand each other somewhat)

High Elvish

The Current dominant Language in the Elbae Sultanat.

Under Elvish

Spoken by the Deepelves or Drow of the Underlands beneath the surface.

Ancient Elvish

One of if not the single oldest language that can be entirely translated and understood to this day, ancient elvish is spoken usually by elven nobility, international speakers are almost always scholars of arcane and divine magic. 

  
  

Meadi Languages(can all reasonably understand each other somewhat)

  

High Meadi

The national Language of Meadrath.

Weali

Spoken by the Blijgrater citizens of the Trinwood slopes.

  

Smallfolk Languages (can all reasonable understand each other somewhat)

Old Gnomish Symbolspeek

A non-verbal Language that is still used within gnomish craftsmanship but almost entirely lost to the world outside of various Ottian Scholars.

Ottian Gnomish

Spoken by almost all Gnomes of the World.

Kortopian Dwarvish

Spoken by the Dwarves of the Kortopi Mountains.

Trollback Dwarvish

Spoken by the Dwarves of the Trollback Mountains.

Mistspire Dwarvish

Spoken by the Dwarves of the Mistspire Mountains.

Halfling Dialects

Spoken by the various Halfling Communities mostly found west of Meadrath.

  

Goblin Languages(can all reasonable understand each other somewhat)

Undergob

Spoken by the Goblins living in the Goblin Hive Cities of the Underlands.

Overgob

Spoken by the Goblins living in the Goblin Caravans travelling the Surface world.

Hobgob

Spoken by the Hobgoblin Clans roaming Surface and Underlands.

  
  
  
  
  
  

Miscellaneous Human Languages

Arevi

Spoken by the tribesmen of the Arevi Yerkir.

Cevoli

The National Language of Battvia and in the Paper Cities.

Kheri

The oldest human Language preceding even Rakhi, spoken in Kherova.

Bykmål Dialects

Spoken by the various peoples of Trykkerog.

Ukioll

Spoken by the Ukioll peoples in and south of the Eldveil

  

Miscellaneous Inhuman Languages

K`ragi

Spoken by the Cyclopi of lost Cyraclion.

Qahori

Spoken by the Orcs of the Qahori Federation.

Satyr

Spoken by the Satyrs of lost Cyraclion.

Nirydian

Spoken by the Kobold Khaganates of eastern Nirydia.

Tallspeak

Spoken by the ogre clans and trolls of the world, related to Giantspeak.

Grippli

Spoken by the various Grippli tribes of the world.

Gnollish

Spoken by the Gnolls of Behera.

  

Exotic and lost Languages  
(cannot be learned by conventional manners, need good reason to have these)

Rakhi

Language of the lost Empire of Rakh, usually only spoken by scholars.

Cyracli

Language of the Torn City, almost no one in the known world speaks this.

Giantspeak

Language of the Giants of the North, only spoken by various druids and scholars in far Grachy and beyond.

  

Draconic

While there are no actual reliable records of communication with Dragons, various dragon cults of Trykkerog and Meadland used to commune in this tongue, which is now exclusively spoken by scholars.

Abyssal

The “Forbidden Tongue” used to commune with Demons and Fiends of the Chaotic Plane, colloquially referred to as the Abyss.

Protean

Spoken by the mysterious residents and entities of the Stagnant Plane.

Celestial

Spoken by Spirits of the Positive Plane, there are few divine scholars that fluently speak this language but its script is used in various ancient religions.

Deadspeak

Spoken by necromancers and invaders from the Negative Plane.

  

Druidic

Spoken by the various druidic circles of the world, some say it is impossible for one to learn this language that is not accepted within a circle.

Sylvan

The language of the Fey and various other Spirits of the material and spiritual realm.

Mertongue

Spoken by the mysterious residents of the Ocean Floors and the Merfolk of the vast Seas, sometimes by Qahori Seafairers.

Aboleth

The mind-warping whispers of the unfathomable horrors of the deepest and darkest corners of the world.

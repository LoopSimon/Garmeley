<p>In the later days of May in the year 927PA, a tense situation nearly became a great tragedy when the Mercenaries of the <a href="Laughing Clouds" class="internal-link">Laughing Clouds Band</a> laid siege to the Shrine of Dimrod, within the Mesovo Hills of eastern <a href="Hainach" class="internal-link">Hainach</a>. Reports of the situation are admittedly varied and largely reciprocated by the reani Dimitri Ipros and Professor Jiballo who both were part of the conflict until its resolution.</p>
<p>The Shrine of Dimrod, also known as the Eye of the Stonefather, lies around half a days ride south of the Caravans Road within the Hills and is a well maintained location of Worship to the premier deity within reani spiritworship. A variety of smaller and largely isolated villages are located deeper within the hills that tend to be ignored by local authorities and live in a mutual understanding with greater civilization around them, these communities guard and maintain the Shrine and its surrounding paths and have done so for generations. The exact origin for the Shrine as a place of worship is unknown but the almadi Professor <a href="Xarvin Jiballo" class="internal-link">Xarvin Jiballo</a>, himself at the centre of the incident, has theorized it to be over 800 years old and of supernatural making. </p>

<p>The office of the Sheriff of Kontini, at the time held by the honourable <a href="Adral Fidrakis" class="internal-link">Adral Fidrakis</a>, has launched an investigation in the ongoings behind the incident, as previously mentioned the Professor himself was contacted and the Sheriff personally conducted an Interview with a villager and former warden at the shine, Dimitry Ipsos. The following report is the best reconstruction emerging as a result of this investigation.</p>
<p>Professor Jiballo travelled through the Hillside of the Mesovo on a journey to Pikris, the Princedoms Capital and had crossed the <a href="Mesovo Border" class="internal-link">Point Meso Bridge</a> on the 11th of May, he hired the services of the Laughing Clouds, headed by their Captain <a href="Hector Razzan" class="internal-link">Razzan</a> in <a href="Mereskis" class="internal-link">Mereskis</a> for security reasons. Fidrakis confirmed the crossing date with the Gelreas Bridge Guard and the credentials of Jiballo as a renowned academic. According to the Professor himself, his trip to the Shrine was one of academic curiosity concerning his current work, a sequel to the  Book "The Golem in religious traditions" published in 919PA. The small group of mercenaries and Jiballo himself would make their way through the Hills, stopping at at least two minor villages and interviewing the local populace to satisfy the Professors curiosity without any major incident before arriving at the Shrine on the 20th of May 927PA.</p>
<p>Here is where the reports widely dissonate with each other: according to Xarvin he and three personal guards made the climb up to the Shrines zenith and were treated unfriendly, upon polite inquires on their side they were threatened and, fearing a shakedown or worse in inhospitable lands, confronted with armed robbers. Viego, a mercenary assigned to Jiballo stepped up and defended the Professor, cutting down an assailant in self defence before being grievously wounded himself, a situation that would later on lead to the mans death in lue of proper care according to Jiballo. Upon this Jiballo, his three guards, as well as fellow visitors to the shrine were taken captive by the natives and refused to provide care for the wounded Mercenary.</p>
<p>The interview with Ipros tells another story: in his memory it was a day like any other were the wardens of the shrine provided education and care for visiting pilgrims while protecting the integrity of the shrines structure. A multilingual guide by the name of Iarel had been communicating with the visitors in beheran and elven when noticing the Professor himself breaking the rules of the Shrine and touching the statue of the Stonefather. When Xarvin supposedly kept ignoring the wardens commands to stop and step away, Iarel and other natives confronted the Professor, calmly and without weapons. Still ignoring the locals, Jiballo apparently did the unthinkable and broke off a piece of stone from the statue for "future reference" upon which Iarel first put his hands on the man and was in turn attacked by Viego who was the first to draw steel. </p>
<p>The Guardian of the Shrine, a wise woman by the name of <a href="Lorana" class="internal-link">Lorana</a> saw herself forced to erect a protective barrier invoking ancient rituals embedded within the shrines structure itself. This barrier acted as a semi-permeable shield of sorts that would protect a small localized area around the Shrine from the surrounding forces of the Laughing Clouds.</p>
<p>Fidrakis Investigation showed that a part of Dimrods ancient statue was indeed destroyed, although whether this happened as a result of Jiballos actions was indeterminable.</p>

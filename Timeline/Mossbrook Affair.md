<p>In January of 923PA, the village of Mossbrook within the Deathcap Mire was destroyed due to the appearance of a mysterious Tower over night on the dawn of spring. The exact persons involved, their motivations and their fate remain vastly unknown.</p>

<p>Mossbrook was a village of fishers and eel farmers with a population of between 150-300 people, they were likely  in contact with a few of the other smaller communities within the Deathcap Mire. Records of the settlement it go back to mid  8th century when Miaragua della Vari, member of the <a href="Excavators Guild of Butera" class="internal-link">Buteran Guild of Excavators</a>, described it during an attempt at establishing inland routes to the northern Rakhian Wasteland. Much of the information we know of Mossbrook today is due to the testimony of one of its survivors, the Halfling Velona Morrington, who had been saved out of Mossbrooks ruins by a party of adventuring heroes and brought across the southern Kortopis to the trinvale village of Highslope.</p>
<p>On the night of the 39th of January of the year 923PA, a large, five story stone tower materialized in the centre of Mossbrook, causing a minor earthquake and a large flood of the surrounding area, the village at its apex, with the exception of the Halfling Velona and the Human Arlen every inhabitant died overnight as a consequence. A party of travellers coming from Ligoggia happened to be en route to Mossbrook to restock on supplies while traversing the Mire, made up of five adventurers and was able to administer first aid to Arlen and Velona.</p>
<p>While Arlen, the Village Elderman, passed away as a consequence of his injuries in the following night, Velona survived the incident thanks to the quick aid by the party. According to the girl, further inspection of the towers upper floors revealed it as a somewhat academic location with a library and student quarters included on its floors. The Party ventured further into the tower and was set upon by what seemed to be a variety of malformed monstrosities, perhaps victims of the so called <a href="The Veilcurse" class="internal-link">Veilcurse</a> following a Ritual gone wrong. The party dispatched the abominations and progressed further until they found themselves at the top of the tower, where they found what Velona described as a strange arcane instrument and another survivor: a young human male by the name of Tomasz.</p>
<p>Velona describes Tomasz as a part of the group that caused the incident, he spoke in a heavy meadi accent and declared himself a Wizard in Training and of financial means, her comments on his personality stray considerably in the negative for obvious reasons. According to the meadi, the Towers transposition was the result of a successful arcane experiment in the realm of Inter-planar Teleportation, the destruction of Mossbrook simply a rounding error and the physical mutations and mental degradation of the rest of his colleagues a consequence of the experimental nature of their calculations.</p>

<p>The exact Fate of Tomasz is unknown, while Velona admits he was taken with the party through the Hollow Road, a canyon within the southern Kortopis, it seems like he fell victim to an accident on the journey, an event the Halfling does not seem willing to share further details on.</p>

<p>Some more observant people have pointed at the mysterious disappearence of a Junior Tower of the estranged meadi Arcanist, <a href="Pavo of Alban" class="internal-link">Archmage Pavo of Alban</a>, within the same timespan. The description of the towers form and Tomaszs meadi clothing and accent point towards this missing Tower being the same one that had materialized within Mossbrook. Attempts to bring this to any court or official within the years following the Tragedy by Velona were shut down or deemed fruitless due an essential total lack of evidence; Mossbrook and the remnants of the Tower were found further destroyed and while the village likely eventually sunk into the Swamp, the stone blocks of the tower remained. Signs of meddling and other sabotage were found as if someone had retro actively sought out the location to get rid of evidence and destroy the structure as good as they could. Without any evidence linking a greater Establishment to the end of her village and the death of her loved ones, Velona would find no hope of greater reconciliation.</p>
<p>The idea of a "disappearing Tower" caused widespread neglect due to the occult and un-scientific research into instant transposition of matter living and inorganic, also known as Teleportation for times immemorial; but suffice it to say that such discoveries would likely be kept secret from the public if anyone was indeed researching the field or had gotten their hands on the results of such studies.</p>

<p>Velona would take on the last name Morrington, the same as the local baker of Highslope, and move on to found and run a successful Cafe in the city of Calmbrook in the southern <a href="Trinvale" class="internal-link">Trinvale</a> , known as Berils Rest, where she found the impressive funds to start such an endeavour is a mystery. </p>

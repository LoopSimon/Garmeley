<p>Gisela IV is the current Queen of Meadrath, the Lady Protector of the <a href="Freelands" class="internal-link">Freelands</a> and the sovereign ruler of <a href="Zmalig" class="internal-link">Zmalig</a>, <a href="Lesmere" class="internal-link">Lesmere</a> and <a href="Gisvale" class="internal-link">Gisvale</a>, she ascended the throne at the age of 13 in the year 910PA.</p>
<p>Born Gisela Para von Kor, she is the daughter of Queen Gisela III and Prince Jean du Jera, after the death of her mother and her coronation she was represented by a Regent Council headed by her father until she assumed full governance in 913 on her 16th birthday. Gisela married Frederick von Mart, son of Karel von Mart the Archduke of Gisvale, in the year 919PA and has given birth to two children, the boys Gildrick and Karel.</p>
<p>The Queen has build a reputation of stoic utilitarianism and strong governance over her reign, quickly stepping out of the shadows of both her late Mother as well as the presence of her Father. While her mother, known to her contemporaries and posterity as "the Kind" was a successful Queen by many standards, hers was a country governed by strong politicians and court politics, many have referred to the High-Chancellor, <a href="Odrick von Fulmer" class="internal-link">Count von Fulmber</a>, as the real power behind the throne of the kind Queen of Meadrath. The conflict between von Fulmer and the Duke du Jera, later the Prince-Consort, dominated the politics of the last decade of Gisela IIIs reign, with du Jeras position as Head of the Regent-Council for the young new monarch weighing the balance in his favour during the first half of the 910s. Count von Fulmer would be banished in all but name from court and lost much of his influence under du Jeras regency, while many considered the Father of the young Queen to retain much of his control and power after her ascendency this was not to be.</p>
<p>The details of Giselas relationship to her Father are unknown, but his quick leave from Korsfall back to Jera, following a greater dismissal of much of the palace staff and the return of exiled dignitaries expressed a clear rift between the Queen and the former Prince-Consort, as well as a sign that this monarch would rule by her own grace and underline the hierarchy between her and any advisors. Be that as it may, the return of the Count von Fulmer and his re-instatement as High-Chancellor of the Realm surprised fewer observers, it seemed like von Fulmer had at least in some way bested his old Rival du Jera after all, although with a much more active and considerate monarch to serve than the simple times of Gisela IIIs court.</p>

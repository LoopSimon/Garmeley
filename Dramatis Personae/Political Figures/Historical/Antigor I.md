<p>Born as the Prince of Kisnau during the days of the Molina Kings of <a href="Rean" class="internal-link">Rean</a>, Antigor would lead the people of Pilvia, later known as the realm of Grevenna, into a bloody but successful war of independence during the early-mid 6th century. His legendary prowess on the battlefield, near invincibility in personal combat and political dominance earned him the nickname "the Bronzefist". Recalling the days before Reani rule, he would shed the title of Prince and declare himself the Grand Lord of Grevenna, referencing the legendary pilvian folk hero Grimus Grevenna who was said to have tricked the old gods into blessing the Pilvian mountains with gold and bronze. </p>
<p>Antigor was tall, especially for a man of his time, and stood at 2 metres tall, which would lead some of his enemies to refer to him as "the Ogre of Kisnau" during the Great War for Independence. He led his troops from the front, relished in physical contest and man to man combat and is often credited for the modern day revival of gladiatorial traditions in southern Ilrakhan. At times he was known to shave his head clean and be painted in a bronze hue, combining with his mysterious and magical armour to invoke the image of a living statue, a man made weapon on the battlefield.</p>
<h3>Great War for Independence</h3>
<p>The Prince of Pilvia before Antigor, his father Prince Karnoman of Kisnau, had died in the year 510PA and Antigor succeeded him at the age of 20, already a respectable warrior and promising candidate. However the King of Rean at the time, Sachmon du Rean, envied the rich Karnoman and cancelled legal documents that would have allowed Antigor to inherit most of his owed fortunes via royal decree. In response to the outraged Antigors appeal for justice Sachmon relished, having planned to successfully bait the young and inexperienced Prince into a political faux-pas and instead of relenting to Antigor, demanded to relinquish vast stretches of his pilvian demesne to Thierry du Rean, Sachmons cousin. Antigor was detained at Morwina and imprisoned by Thierry only to be freed by pilvian nationalist supporters and declaring vengeance upon the House of du Rean, essentially marking the start of what would later become the Grevennan War of Independence. </p>
<p>It took 12 years of war, countless dead, too many battles to name and the death of Sachmon, his son Giraud as well as Theirry of the du Rean line, but in the year 530PA Antigor would achieve victory and declare himself Grand Lord of a free Pilvia, naming his new free realm Grevenna in the honour of the mythical Grimus Grevenna. At this point many pilvians had been culturally closer to the vihri of the north in many regards than to the Reani of the west, including their choice of faith: a majority of the new country was Gethric and the worship of the Prophet became a symbol of unity for the country with Antigor himself announcing his conversion towards the middle of the war. The countries of Vihr and Grevenna would only grow closer together over the following centuries, united in not only religion but a common enemy to the south as well...</p>
<p>Antigor I, the Bronzefist of House Rofea, son of Karnoman and first Grand Lord of Grevenna would drown at the age of 68 under somewhat disputed circumstances during a storm while journeying towards Thulmouth; His body was never found and the legendary Bronze Armor of Antigor never officially recovered. The Grand Lord was succeeded by his eldest daughter, Elena Rofea and today, nearly 450 years later the pilvian peoples of Grevenna are still ruled by his direct blood descendant, Caroman Rofea.</p>

<p>Born in the City of Morwina, Grevenna in the year 886 as the second child to the locally famous horse trader Lian Valvasor, Janez Vajkard Valvasor was soon send to Kisnau to study in the sciences and receive an education. Many details pertaining his early academic career or his youth in general are unknown but he quickly showed an interest in basic alchemy and magecraft, eventually earning him an apprenticeship at then Reanport School for Magicks under the famous Scholar Korman van Schipwig. Valvasor would pursue Realmatic Studies at Patwin Tower following his education in Reanport and slowly drift away from applied and theoretical Kynosis. In 917, he rejoined with his old Mentor van Schipwig to help him finish his lifelong work “The Fractal Bridge” and remain at the School as its Third Seat for almost 6 years teaching Harlinic Space an advanced conditions. Janez Vajkard Valvasor would serve 4 terms and retire from the position, his current whereabouts are unknown.</p>
<p>Valvasors research into the Connection between Realmatic or Harlinic & Planar Energies and later on his theoretical discovery of a Null-Field have earned him international respect. His visits to Karajal have been frequent and were criticized by members of the northern academic establishment. His love for the alchemical process and kynotic theory never left him, and he was said to have been one of the few scholars that have had personal debates with the mysterious and unknowable Beheran Alchemist Gynla Pemo, before the latter's disappearance. </p>

<p>Following the outbreak of the <a href="Garmian Succession Crisis" class="internal-link">Garmian Civil War</a> Valvasor vocally supported the southern cause, encouraging his former collegues at Patwin Tower in their cause and criticizing the <a href="Orenburg Pact" class="internal-link">Orenburg Pacts</a> relevance in modern times. His speech at Reanport in 295, disavowing all scholars and mages fighting for Coster of Melmbirth and his alliance in view of their pillaging of eastern Garmeley coupled with his disappearance a year later from public eye are the subject of various conspiracy theories. Nevertheless his efforts and support of Patwin Tower and the southern Alliance started a wave of academic uproar that initially  was conflated with the general academic silent revolution against the wermian accords but later on became a more humanitarian reaction of international academics  against the escalating war.</p>

<p>Gisela II was a Queen of <a href="Meadrath" class="internal-link">Meadrath</a> and <a href="Gisvale" class="internal-link">Gisvale</a> from 704PA until her death in 772PA. She is to this date the longest reigning monarch in ilrakhi human history at over 68 years of rule, her eventful reign, and its effects on modern day <a href="Meadland" class="internal-link">Meadland</a> and Ilrakhan often put her in contention as the "ideal Queen". She is famously remembered for the Giselan Reforms, her strong stance against Gethric Pogroms and support of the <a href="Trinicism" class="internal-link">Trinicism</a> cause, the expansion of the <a href="Korsfall College" class="internal-link">Korsfall College</a> as perhaps the single most important human institution for arcane research and the unification and centralization of the Realm itself.</p>
<p>Gisela was the eldest surviving child of Gildher II and Margaret von Jera, and thusly the third Monarch of the von Kor Dynasty founded by her Grandfather, King Gildher I. Following the untimely death of her father during a hunting accident, Gisela was crowned as Queen of Meadrath, Korsfall and Gisvale in the tradition of her two predecessors in the Moonlight Palace at Korsfall. Her younger brother, Prince Karil von Kor was only 2 years of age when she ascended the throne and he became heir-apparent himself.</p>
<h3>Internal Conflicts</h3>
<p>While Meadrath had been consolidated as the union of the Kingdoms of Korsfall and Gisvale and various Duchies and Counties decades earlier in 664PA, her father, aunt and grandfather had struggled to keep the realm as a unified force, specifically running into struggles with autonomous and rebellious vassals. The Court at Korsfall was weak in the years following Giselas Accession, and a faction of Dukes and Counts gathered at Dellweid in the Duchy of <a href="Lesmere" class="internal-link">Lesmere</a>, holding what could amount to essentially a second court and refusing to acknowledge Gisela's authority in large parts. This situation would culminate in the Lesmerey demand, for Gisela to marry Duke Otto von Praum or essentially be deposed. What is now known as the Praum-Affair ended in the clsoe avoidance of martial conflict, the arrest and execution of ringleaders like von Praum himself and the reorganization of old feudal vassal contracts in a more centralized fashion. Many historians agree that the Queen-Mother, Margaret von Jera, was instrumental in the decisive political victory and adept handling of the situation. Gisela would later marry Count Alberic von Lot in 712PA.</p>
<h3>Civil Reforms</h3>
<p>Gisela instituted a vast array of economic, legal and military reforms during her almost seven decade long reign as monarch. While posterity often focuses on her civil reforms to end serfdoms most cruel practices, it would be false to paint her as champion of the commonfolk as most of her changes to the status of serfs in Meadrath were economically motivated, ensuring better living conditions and an overall healthier and more educated population and workforce.</p>
<p>Gisela largely abolished most uses of judicial torture from the common courts and limited which crimes could be punished by death, banned trials by ordeal and reformed the judicial system of decentralized courts and judges following different legal codes making the system more efficient and beholden to not only a central authority at Korsfall but began works on unifying the legal codes, a work that would be finished soon after her death and can largely be attributed to the Queens efforts. Her initially most dangerous and controversial choice was the removal of special courts for special social classes, specifically the gethric code, in this way she pre-emanated trinicist movements by a few decades and acted as a leading example of other states that disavowed Iztaws legal authority within their borders, following the Schism.</p>
<p>Her close relationship and exclusive patronage of the College of Korsfall, including special commissions, exclusive independent grants, powerful advisory positions at court and within the judicial apparatus and legal authority over all occult and arcane practices of the country is often seen as the reason for the prolonged dominance of Korsfall as an Institution of arcane research, teaching and discovery on the world stage. Her direct involvement in the Ichorist Choir and its founding is unknown, but it is almost certain that she was at least aware and tolerating of the practice, if not fully supportive.</p>
<h3>Gethric Schism</h3>
<p>The Queen actively disavowed the Gethric Pogroms against elves of the High-Bisop of Iztaw as politically motivated barbarianism by a puppet Bisop under the Control over the Vihri Crown, joining a large faction of monarchs, clerics and scholars that were not at all involved in the east-ilrakhani power struggle between Vihr and the Sultanate. Following the attempt of the Silver Wheel to forcefully disband the Council of Trinfurt in 764PA, the Queen joined the Trinicist party, actively supporting local militias against gethric violence and enacted  purges within the meadi clergy, branding many church officials as traitors to the faith and the crown, exiling or in some cases executing them. Giselas harsh stance for Trinicism signalled to neighbouring countries of lesser power and influence that disavowing Iztaw and Vihri dominance over the faith was not only possible but welcomed and, coupled with the ongoing military conflict occupying Urlander and Vihrmen nations,  led to the political schism between Trinicist and Gethricists udring the 2nd half of the 8th century PA.</p>

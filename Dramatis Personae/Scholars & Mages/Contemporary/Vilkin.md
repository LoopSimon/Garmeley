<p>Roizar Vilkin of Edessa Tower is one of the most published  Academics alive today and the Father of the so thusly named Vilkins Model, that describes the relationship of all theoretical Planes with each other and our own world, it builds upon thousands of years of academic research into Planar Theory and is the widely accepted most accurate model of pure Planar Cosmology we have available to date. </p>
<p>Vilkin was born in Kisnau the eldest son of a Carpenter and earned a scholarship at the Kisnau University of Scienes where he excelled at Mathematics & Physics as well as displaying an affinity to the Arcane. He went on to further his studies of the planes at Reanport University and later learned under the infamous Mirai herself, Carassa al-Kael at <a href="Karajal School of Insight" class="internal-link">Karajal</a> in the Sultanate. <br>In the Year 899PA at the age of 30 he became a Magister at Edessa Tower and 4 years later in 903PA succeeded Timora of Falb as the acting Archmage. In his position as Archmage he would finalize his magnum Opus and publish the famous "Forces in Motion" describing his universal Model of Planar Relativism in 918PA. </p>
<p>As a consequence of the encroaching political turmoil around Edessa between the Lordship of Grevenna in the south and the Rascy zi Vihr in the north, an older Vilkin would renounce his position as Archmage, leaving his oldest student and colleague Pavel Rigoran in the position while taking up a position as Professor at the Reanport School for Magicks, in a non teaching role, he resides there to this day.</p>
<p>Vilkin became an avid opponent of the <a href="Luminists" class="internal-link">luminist</a> Movement that rose in popular amongst arcane scholars during the 9th century, proclaiming himself a staunch defender of the Wermian Accords and warning of the dangers of uncontrolled and unsanctioned planar summoning and experimental Prism structures in general. This attitude has put him at odds with <a href="Janez Vajkard Valvasor" class="internal-link"> Janez Valvasor</a>, a fellow Professor at Reanport.</p>

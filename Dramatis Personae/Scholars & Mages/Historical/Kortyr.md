<p>Kortyr, sometimes referred to as "the Kortyr" or simply "Paleblood" was a near legendary spiritual leader in southern Vihr and Pilvia during the 1st and 2nd century PA. He amassed a sizeably following during the times of the <a href="Vihrmen Federation" class="internal-link">Vihri Federation of Tribes</a>, and led a cult of radical <a href="Turvik Paganism" class="internal-link">turvik</a> followers that eventually grew into a fanatical sect following Kortyrs every command. It is unclear wether or not Kortyr is the true name of Paleblood or was simply a regional title for a man of his position within the local turvik cults that became synonymous with the half-elf due to his infamous activities.</p>
<p>Kortyr is most famously remembered for his quest of immortality, that included grand scale sacrifices, mass ritual suicide and his famous practice of Hemomancy, the art of Blood Magic. His only remaining piece of writing is the Liturfestus, also known as the Manifest of Kortyr or the Book of Blood, whose few surviving copies have been banned almost universally by most official institutions of magic but also greatly advanced later research into the fields of healing magic and the nature of planar energy and its sources, it is speculated that there are fewer than five surviving copies of the work.</p>
<p>His fascination with the unicorn, a majestic animal native to the vihri expanse that was revered in vihrmen culture and religious practice of eastern turvik paganism, led Kortyr to conduct great and brutal experiments with the animals, him believing that the horns of the creatures contained a divine spark of life, that could offer the key for his selfish quest. Palebloods great hunts for the beasts is likely a major reason for the eventual steep decline  of the unicorn population on Inara, and he is often portrayed as a creature of darkness tainting the traditionally seen as holy and good unicorn within clerical iconography of the later centuries. </p>
<p>The fate of Kortyr and his eventual demise are shrouded in mystery, the general consensus is that he was betrayed during a great ritual or procession and buried alive but considering his near mythical status as a man closer to a fairytale villain than a historical figure within many pilvian and vihri tales, theories about him having achieved immortality and still lying in his tomb somewhere, or his soul having been transferred to his acolytes among many others have always enjoyed great popularity within folklore.</p>

<p>Jevana Loxley, commonly referred to as Jevana of Leston was a 8th & 9th century Wizard and Mathematician, mainly concerned with Leygrid Theory, she is most famous for developing the so called Leyprism-Model that seeks to improve upon classical Fluxbridge Theory and the elven Leygrid Model. Her treaties on the Leyprism caused a re-evaluation of ancient casting principles and would be universally embraced in elven, gnomish and human communities. </p>
<p>Jevana was notoriously courted by King Gandry II of <a href="Gelmsburg" class="internal-link">Gelmsburg</a> whose advances she rejected multiple times, often considered to be the reason for her eventual parting with the ancient circle of Gelmsburg and later life at Greenstone Tower in the outskirts of <a href="Ak-Saray" class="internal-link">Ak-Saray</a>. She studied under the great Zovac Harlini from 778-793PA, shortly before his mysterious vanishing. Her own pupil, meadi Sorceress Muriel von Gloss, would praise her work and advancements life long and even name the library of <a href="Korsfall College" class="internal-link">Korsfall College</a> of which she was Rector at the time, the Jevana Wing in 879PA. </p>
<p>In the year 817PA, as acting Archmage of Greenstone Tower, she came together with eight other leaders in the realm of magical scholarship and excellence to form the <a href="Orenburg Pact" class="internal-link">Orenburg Pact</a>, as a founding member she and Greenstone Tower as an Institution are remembered as part of the so called Orenburg Nine. The Pact seeks to prevent the near apocalyptic effect of unleashed mages on the battlefield, demonstrated for all by the actions of <a href="Jurahim al-Biad" class="internal-link">Jurahim al-Biad</a> that essentially ended the decades long conflict and destroyed the Isthmus at Horad. Jevana was a fervent supporter of the idea and a lifelong pacifist, the actions of her aforementioned student von Gloss, who would controversially break the pact in the eyes of many, were contrasted by her teachers staunch stance against the use of spell casting in times of war.  </p>

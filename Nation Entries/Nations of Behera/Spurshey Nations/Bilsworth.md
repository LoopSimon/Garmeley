<p>The indubitable astounding and unprecedent notion of a realm of the undead has been realized for better or worse within the Bilsworth Ministry. Following the Disaster of 747PA almost the entire population of the <a href="Spursley" class="internal-link">Kingdom of Spursley</a> was afflicted with a self-replicating planar curse, exact origins unknown that led to them obtaining a unique state of undeath. In current time this realm that is in so many ways unlike any other is managed and ruled by the Ministry from the City of Rigsbridge.</p>
<p>The Unliving of Bilsworth are almost all unable to leave the restraints of their country, the Node-Towers constructed by the Mages of Spursley act as a sphere of magic that both grants the citizens of Bilsworth eternal live but also spans the Region as an invisible barrier, a near force field that will not let anyone under the effect of the Curse leave its bounds. Nevertheless, certain members of the Ministry have managed to develop magical processes to supress the curse temporarily, effectively allowing someone to die for the duration of the suppression, only to be re-animated later. This is the only Peace the people of Bilsworth know and access to this "little death" has become the only relevant currency within the country.</p>
<p>Trade and contact with outsiders is limited but not impossible, those unaffected by the Curse can enter and leave the Spheres freely if they chose to: Bilsworth is bordered to the north-east and East by the Deep Jungles of Vinjira and the Vinjira Mountains, where members of the serpentfolk citizens of Yokosch sometimes journey to the Ministry; travelling south eventually one will reach the Realm of <a href="Marzun" class="internal-link"> Marzun</a> , humans ruled by the <a href="Minotaur" class="internal-link">Minotaur</a>  Lords that overthrew the ancient Empire of <a href="Geol" class="internal-link">Geol</a> ; on its West is the Sea of Clouds, only a few Node Towers were built on small Isles in the coastal areas, making seafairing a limited endeavour for the citizens of Bilseworth, but the old cousins of the Ministry, surviving remnants of Spursley overseas that have organised themselves into a republic, the country of Runsby, have claimed true inheritance of Spursely and treat Bilsworth as a careful future prospect of retuning conquest, albeit trade still occurs from time to time. </p>
<h2>Administration</h2>
<p>The Bilsworth Ministry operates as a highly centralised Magocracy. Unlike traditional feudal nations that divide land based on geography or noble lineages, Bilsworth's administrative borders are strictly defined by the overlapping magical radii of the ancient Node Towers. Because the unliving population cannot physically leave these fields, the Ministry has organised the state to maximize arcane efficiency, border defense, and the distribution of the Little Death. The nation is divided into two primary tiers of administration: Directorates on a regional level and Wards on a local level.</p>
<h4>The Directorates</h4>
<p>Bilsworth is divided into five primary Directorates, sometimes colloquially called Spheres. Each Directorate is governed by a  Governor appointed directly by High Minister William Bradford.</p>
<h5>Rigsbridge</h5>
<p>The Rigsbridge central Directorate is the political, economic, and magical heart of Bilsworth. Encompassing the modern capital and its immediate surrounding lands, this Directorate boasts the most well maintained Node Towers. It is home to the High Ministry and the grand arcane academies where the suppression of the curse is researched and refined. Its Regional Capital is Rigsbridge.</p>
<h5>Wainbree</h5>
<p>The Wainbree Heritage Sector is densely populated but largely dilapidated and encompasses the former capital of the fallen Kingdom of Spursley. Because the old capital took the brunt of the 747 PA Disaster, the husks here are numerous and often impoverished. The Ministry maintains heavy magical oversight here to prevent mass hysteria and carefully regulates the high demand for the little death. Its Regional Capital is Wainbree.</p>
<h5>The Vinjiran March</h5>
<p>Bordering the Deep Jungles of Vinjira and the Vinjira Mountains, this eastern territory serves as Bilsworth's primary diplomatic and trade zone. Unaffected outsiders, specifically the <a href="Safir" class="internal-link">Safir</a> of <a href="Yokosch" class="internal-link">Yokosch</a>, often journey here to trade goods for ancient Spursley relics or arcane knowledge. The Node Towers here are calibrated for defensive projection. Its Regional Capital is Bramhill.</p>
<h5>Littoral</h5>
<p>Comprising the western coastline along the Sea of Clouds and the few offshore islands that possess functioning Node Towers. Seafaring is an incredibly dangerous and limited endeavour for the unliving, so the Cloud Coast is heavily monitored to prevent citizens from sailing beyond the Node Fields and suffering eternal, agonising immobilisation in the deep ocean. It also serves to watch for ships from their rival successor state, Runsby. Its regional Capital is Dunpool.</p>
<h5>The Reach</h5>
<p>The heavily fortified southern border separating Bilsworth from the Realm of Marzun. The Minotaur Lords view the undead of Bilsworth with deep suspicion bordering sometimes on outright hostility. The owers here are highly militarized, overlapping tightly to allow unliving soldiers to patrol the borders without risk of stepping out of the magical boundary.</p>
<h4>The Wards</h4>
<p>Below the Directorates, the country is subdivided into Node Wards, often simply referred to as Wards.  
A Ward is defined by the exact territorial radius of a single functioning Node Tower, with each Ward being overseen by a Tower Magistrate, usually a mid ranking mage of the Ministry.</p>
<p>The Magistrate is responsible for the upkeep of their specific Tower and, most importantly, the operation of the local Cessation Asylum: the bureaucratic facility where citizens come to spend their earned currency to undergo the magically induced little death.</p>
<p>Where the magical fields of two Towers overlap, so called Conjunction Zones are formed. These areas are heavily trafficked commercial hubs, as they offer redundancy; if one tower fails or undergoes maintenance, citizens in the Conjunction Zone will not immediately succumb to the agonising effects of a failing field.</p>
<h2>Religion and Internal Security</h2>
<p>While traditional Doggerism historically demands equal reverence for all seasonal deific aspects of Lorel, Vanter, Wissger, and Kallag, the disaster of 747PA fundamentally altered the theology within Bilsworth. <br>Today, Kallag, the Lord of Bones, has been elevated to the absolute prime deity of the state. However, the priesthood of Kallag does not operate independently, instead the magocracy, the clergy has been entirely absorbed into the government, functioning as the Ministry of Internal Peace and Harmony.</p>
<p>At this point far from a traditional church, this Instititution acts as Bilsworth's propaganda arm and secret police. The priests of Kallag walk the streets ensuring "spiritual compliance." They monitor the populace for signs of mental degradation, gather information on anti-government dissent, and preach that High Minister Bradford's control over the little death is not a bureaucratic monopoly, but a holy sacrament granted by Kallag himself.</p>
<h2>Arcane Infrastructure and Field Decay</h2>
<p>The entire existence of Bilsworth relies on the continuous operation of the Node Towers, because of this, so called engineers are the most highly valued citizens in the Ministry.</p>
<p>If a Node malfunctions, is sabotaged, or its field begins to wane, the results are catastrophic. The unliving caught within a failing field do not simply freeze or crumble to dust. Instead, they experience Field Decay: an agonizing state of constant de-animation and re-animation. As the magical tether stutters, their bodies attempt to violently decay into corpses, only for the flickering magic to instantly and forcefully knit their rotting flesh and bone back together. This loop of flesh tearing and repairing causes unimaginable agony. Consequently, the defense, maintenance, and power supply of the Node Towers dictate every single military and economic policy the High Minister enacts.</p>

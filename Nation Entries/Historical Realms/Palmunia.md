<p>The Palmuni civlization, were an Inaran people who formed on of the first major civilisations of the Bronze Age in western Behera. The culture likely originated from the proto Beheran tribes that settled in the Lameidus and the Delta of the River Urza, there they formed a series of polities, including petty Kingdoms and militant theocracies, as well as the later dominant Empire of Palmunis, formed around the city of Palmunis, whose walls can be dated back before 2350BA.</p>
<p>Prior to the rise of Rakh, Palmunis was the dominant civilisation in western Behera and is our main source of information for the northern tribes of the Ilrakhan subcontinent during antiquity, whom Palmuni sources refer to as "barbarians and wilderpeople". The Palmuni were in active exchange with the Cyclopi of Cyraclion including larger scale conflicts in the 1st millenium BA, once both the Empire of Palmunis and united <a href="Cyraclion" class="internal-link">Cyraclion</a> had formed out of initially largely independent subcultures and cities.</p>
<p>During the later years of the Empire, Palmunis eventually splintered back into essentially a loose federation of petty cities, as the later Emperors lost almost all powers and turned into religious icons rather than political leaders. It was this decline that would see the younger <a href="Rakh" class="internal-link">rakhi</a> civilisation eventually eclipse and subsume independent Palmunis in their earlier decades of expansionism. As the rakhi were essentially an osmosis of cyracli and proto-rakhi ilrakhani culture many rakhi traditions both administrative and religiously are essentially pastiches of the palmuni. Ironically, due to the destructive nature of the mysterious fall of the Rakhian Empire following the Eruption of Al-Akka in the year 0PA, there are more records and physical evidence remaining of ancient Palmunis than of its greater rakhi successor. In the modern day, the lands of the Urza River are home to the <a href="Bassara" class="internal-link">Marival of Bassara</a>, that has adopted many ancient palmuni traditions within its unique and isolated realm.</p>

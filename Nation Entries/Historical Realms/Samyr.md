<p>Samyr was an ancient Marival named after its capital city of Samyr, modern day Mereskis, that ruled over the Asefi peoples and the lands of northern Behera during much of the Iron Age. A large part of the Marivals culture and history is surprisingly preserved to this day, including many of its oldest buildings. Over the course of its history, the Asefi had never managed to regain political independence following the Fall of the Marival, first being conquered by the Meter <a href="Elven Dynasties" class="internal-link">Dynasty</a>, then the Kings of <a href="Rean" class="internal-link">Rean</a> and later again the Elbae <a href="Elbae" class="internal-link">Elbae</a>.</p>
<p>The Marival, a term applied to it mostly in retrospective history, politically consolidated in 712BA, following the so called "Great Marriage" during which the influential cities of Samyr, Erun and Sabrad agreed to stand together politically and economically under the leadership of the Great Temple of Samyr, and later a long line of Queens of all cities. When the armies of Pharaoh Meter II laid siege and raised the Walls of ancient Samyr and Jezanis the last Queen of the Marival perished, the nation as an autonomous political entity officially ended, although it continues to exists to this day as a cultural union of the Asefi peoples.</p>

<p>The Great Kingdom of Urland, named after the region of Urland between the Kortopi and Trollback mountains, was a feudal Kingdom that existed in the Silver and Steel Ages. With strong chivalric and martial traditions it was at times the strongest political entity on the face of Ilrakhan but would succumb to a splintering of its Urien subcultures following the Fall of West Urland, when the Hobgoblins of <a href="Ak-Saray" class="internal-link">Ak-Saray</a> invaded at the end of the 7th century. Conflicts arose earlier when the old faith of the urien peoples, belief in the Turvik Gods, was slowly but surely supplanted by the emerging <a href="Gethricism" class="internal-link">Gethric Church.</a></p>
<p>Political consolidation of Urland was gradual and never fully complete, with major Dukes holding personal courts and even often establishing individual currencies and legal codes at times, although it is nearly impossible to brush over 600 years of urien history over a generalising comb like that. Periods of strong rulers like John II or Idward I saw Urland dominating Ilrakhan as one of, if not the single strongest and most influential nation of the continent. The End of the Dunling Dynasty in power in the late 5th century is where most people contest the eventual cracks in the realm to originate from, although that as well is easier said than proven considering powerful monarchs in the following centuries. King Versil I  became the first of the of Rassel Kings following the Dunlings demise and his line would rule over Urland until the apocalyptic invasion of the Kortopi Hobogblins at the end of the 7th century PA.</p>

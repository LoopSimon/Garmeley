<p>The Aerlian Empire, often simply referred to as Aerlan was a massive Empire originating in eastern Sertovis, at its height it ruled large parts of southern <a href="Othirdon" class="internal-link">Othirdon</a> and the entire Sertovi Peninsula. During the so called "Age of disgrace", 388PA until 605PA, the sheer enormity of the empire, combined with increasing civil unrest, movements of diverging religion and cultures led to its eventual collapse. Almost all of the modern Sertovi tribes are formed from the descendants of the Aerli Tribes, and the Empire is remembered fondly in some while cursed defiantly in others. </p>
<p>The 4th century PA, saw large movements of Neo-Kalbinists and Halfling supremacists organize and grow strong in western Sertovis causing multiple break aways of autonomous territories and cities. The Empire was unable to effectively contain this civil unrest due to the massive natural disaster that struck northern Sertovis and central Othirdon in 388PA, known as the Wrath of Isara and often rumoured to have been more than just an earthquake if various conspiratorial theories.</p>
<p>The consequences of the Wrath of Isara, combined with civil unrest in the far west, the rise of contesting Othiri powers across the Visian Sea and the growing influence of the cevoli settlers from <a href="Martella" class="internal-link">Martella</a> would all be culminating factors during the 4th and 5th century PA, seeing the once mighty Empire fall and eventually disband. With the failed campaign of Emperor Renon III against the united forces of the rebellious nation of <a href="Nahr" class="internal-link">Nahr</a> in western Sertovis, rebel forces would seize the capital city of Mirkard in the year 605PA and declare the Free Kingdom of the Ravi, later known as <a href="Ravland" class="internal-link">Ravland</a>, effectively ending the Aerlian Empire.</p>
<p>The modern day Kingdom of <a href="Erlmondy" class="internal-link">Erlmondy</a> is often seen as the closest cultural link to old Aerlan, proclaiming itself as a successor to the Empire and upholding many of its traditions and values.</p>

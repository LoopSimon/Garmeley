<p>Following the Khulhki-Rebellion of the late 7th century PA, tens of thousands of Shirin Loyalists followed Timpo, Khulkis nephew, northwards into the tundra of Gellions Folly where they would proclaim a new nation of free Shirin, rejecting the oppressive Twintail and the worship of Lakal. Today Vigzil is a larger realm within northern Nirydia and has largely adopted Zuhuri traditions, still openly opposing the Imperium in all major conflicts on the international stage.</p>
<h2>Foundational History</h2>
<p><small><i>for a more detailed explanation of the Rebellion and its Lead up see <a href="Twintail Imperium" class="internal-link">Lisil III and the Khulki Rebellion (670 PA – 695 PA)</a></i></small></p>
<p>The military defeat of the Khulki Rebellion did not extinguish the movement it represented. In the immediate aftermath of the rebellion's collapse, Timpo, nephew of Khulki and the most prominent surviving leader of the Zuhirist faction, organized the withdrawal of the rebellion's committed religious core northward rather than accept reintegration into an Imperium they regarded as irredeemably corrupt. The exodus drew tens of thousands of Shirin loyalists including families, scholars, clergy, and former fighters, traced a difficult route into the subarctic tundra region known of  Gellion's Folly.</p>
<p>The decision to withdraw rather than surrender or continue fighting was Timpo's most consequential act, and has echoed across Nirydian political history for more than two centuries. In the short term it removed the most obstructive element of the rebellion from the Imperium's territory, allowing Yoroy I's post-rebellion reforms to proceed without the ongoing insurgency that would otherwise have continued. In the long term it established a permanent Shirin state, eventually consolidated as the realm of Vigzil, whose foundational identity was constituted by rejection of both the Twintail Imperium and Lakalism simultaneously, and whose adoption of Zuhirist traditions as a state religion gave it an ideological alignment directly opposed to everything the Imperium represented.</p>
<p> Vigzil's emergence as an important realm within northern Nirydia, and its consistent alignment against the Imperium in every major international conflict since its foundation, represents perhaps the most durable consequence of the Lisil Dynasty's mismanagement, a geopolitical problem created by theological overreach that no subsequent military or diplomatic effort has resolved, and which the modern day Imperium continues to confront as an enduring feature of the continental order.</p>
<p>The question of the Zuhirist exile community that had departed for the Folly under Timpo presented a different order of challenge for the Imperium back home. Yoroy I's approach was characteristically pragmatic. He made no attempt to pursue or coerce the exiles, correctly judging that the attempt would consume resources the Imperium could not spare and produce martyrs whose memory would be more politically damaging than the departure of the exiles themselves. The formal position of the Yoroy court toward what would become Vigzil was one of deliberate ambiguity, neither recognizing it as a legitimate sovereign state nor expending effort to deny its existence. An ambiguity that later reigns would find increasingly difficult to maintain as Vigzil consolidated and became an undeniable presence in northern Nirydia.</p>

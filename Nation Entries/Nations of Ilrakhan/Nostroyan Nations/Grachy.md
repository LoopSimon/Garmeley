<p> With the end of the Royal Line of Grach in the early 9th century PA, the influence of the feudal nobility within the Cities of Grachy had almost completed given way to the rich burgher class that emerged during the 7th and 8th century. A proud history and stern Gethric realm still, the young Republic has survived a long tale of struggles already, its wars with <a href="Vihr" class="internal-link">Vihr</a> to the east have left it without its oldest ally standing in the long conflict with the Pehki of <a href="Ukiolla" class="internal-link">Ukiolla</a>.</p>
<h2>Geography</h2>
<p>Grachy is located in southern and central Nostroya, on the eastern shores of the Torn Sea, its coastal regions possess a more maritime climate with cooler summers and milder winters, while northern and eastern parts of the country exhibit a more continental climate with warmer summers and harsher winters. </p>
<p>Grachy is bordered by the Grachhyll in the south and the Mistspires in the East, it stretches as far north as the Stillwater River before the Ukiollan border begins. Forests dominate Grachy substantially but flatlands and farmlands are still to be found, especially within the central Gasyol Region of the country, a vast majority of the countries population lives in the southern coastal areas and around the Halgior River.</p>

<h2>Demographics</h2>
<p>Grachy is dominated by the Grachy people, of vihrmen descent who make up around 82% of the countries total population. </p>
<p>Grachy is notable for its division in class, although they pride themselves on their "democratic" processes and decentralized autonomous regions, essentially only the boyars, rich burghers and higher nobles have a say. The lesser nobility, as well as the higher and more powerful lords often times are vastly preferred by local laws and customs being able to treat peasents like livestock in the worst cases. the beourgoise and academic elite is essentially only present in the coastal cities and south of the Halgior River, the lands of central nostroya are ruled by the ancient boyars in a cruel fashion of feudalism.</p>
<h2>Economy</h2>
<p>Southern Nostroya is rich in natural resources, including a vibrant mining industry in the south that can boast about some of the largest rare mineral extraction operations in the known world where dangerous ores are wrought from the Grachyll. As is common everywhere in Inara, Agriculture is the predominant economy in Grachy too with the country producing a large amount of crops, predominantly grain, and livestock in its southern and coastal areas, Forestry and Fishing are the other large scale industries in grachy.</p>
<p>As opposed to its older brother in the west Vihr and the druidic lands of northern Ukiolla in the north, Grachy has always had to wrestle with a harsh and unforgiving country but has recently enjoyed an extreme influx of soil fertility in its arable lands. The exact reason for this phenomena is unknown and has become the topic of increasing interest for various other nations.</p>
<p>The largest ports within Grachy are Voblag and Durilsk who are both within the mercantile network of the <a href="Trazlov Consortium" class="internal-link">Consortium</a>, as such they enjoy many imports and exports from central Ilrakhan and the ports of Trykkerog. The autonomous and largely self-governing nature of the Grachy Cities has led to a silent mercantile revolution in its more liberal and trade-orientated towns and cities: Following the end of the Kingdom of Halgrach the aristocracy and feudal structures lost their iron grip on power and coin within the country more and more and an increase focus on profit and free trade is supported by the emerging Burgher Class within the country.</p>
<p>Trazlov, sometimes called "old Grach", is deeply connected with the country in many ways, both historically and politically but most importantly economically. It is both the home to the eponymous Consortium as well as various other international Guilds, prominently the so called <a href="The Alchemists Guild" class="internal-link">Men of Glass</a>, the continents largest Alchemical Institution. The city relies not only on trade from the east but also on valuable shipments of Grachian products, primarily rare substances such as phosphorite, pitchblende, and <a href="Graccum" class="internal-link">Graccum</a>.</p>
<h2>Politics</h2>
<p>After its reformation out of the Kingdom of Halgrach, the Ushkyn Faction of Magnates, nobles and burghers assembled within the Blue Palace in Halgrach and declared the Holy Ushkyn Republic of Grachy, often simply referred to as Grachy or Grachy Republic.</p>
<p>The Ushkyn is an old institution originating in the initial traders that sailed and settled across the Horn itself, literally meaning "peoples assembly" in Grachi. As an Institution it is present in almost all towns and cities in the country, and allows all dubush or "honoured people" of the community to partake and vote on certain issues and officials. Generally most burghers, nobles and respected landowners of higher standings are granted the rank of dubush,  although a large majority of commoners do not posses it.</p>
<p>The Grand Ushkyn meets every year in Halgrach and is responsible for major decisions regarding the countries policies, internal and external, as well as the election of the Bagram who leads the country in most matters and acts as head of the Grand Ushkyn, currently the Bagram of Grachy is Ilja Zemov who had been elected to his 2nd term in 922PA. </p>
<p>The Nobility of Grachy, often referred to as the Boyars still hold the vast majority of the power within the country, dominating the Ushkyn of a large amount of regions, during the Reformation of Grachy and the dissolution of the monarchy itself these lesser nobles actually gained power as the country became very decentralized and locally autonomous, as opposed to a more strict feudal structure of absolute rule from Halgrach. The leaders of a city, almost always Boyars themselves are referred to as Grams, they can be seen as equivalent to the most powerful nobles of similar countries and hierarchies. </p>

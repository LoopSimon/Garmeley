<p>One of the smaller realms on the stage of Ilrakhan, Grevenna sits between the Giants of the Vihri Kingdom and the elven Sultanate. The recent decades have not been kind to the men of the Pilv as the opening of the Horad Isthmus a the end of the <a href="Wars of Elbae Aggression" class="internal-link">Elbae Wars</a> would render its land routes from Reanport and Grevenna to the Pilvian Gulf far less profitable than the newly established maritime path. Nevertheless Grevenna is a land of proud history and traditions that strongly values its hard fought independence from man and elf alike.</p>
<h2>Etymology</h2>
<p>Grevenna takes its name from the mythical pilvian adventurer and folk hero, Grimus Grevenna. Subject of many tales and stories, Grimus is said to have wrought Gold and Copper from arrogant gods, bested Fey at Drink and Game and danced with elemental lords. To cement pilvian nationalism and cultural unity in the face of reani oppression, Antigor the Bronzefist, first Grand Lord of Grevenna named the new realm after Grimus in 530PA.</p>

<h2>Geography</h2>
<p>Grevenna is dominated by the Pilv and Opuzna rivers and borders both the Stormy and Sunny Sea in its south, its north is home to the Edessa mountains and within central Grevenna lie the Ebonhills, a mountainous and hilly region exceptionally rich in rare mineral deposits.</p>
<p>The Region of Pilvia is mostly subtropical, featuring a mediterranean climate, with sunny, hot, dry summers and mild winters. Winter temperatures are moderated by the Stormy and Sunny Sea; days of frost are rare. Kisnau and the southern Opuzna are sheltered by the Edessa Mountains north of Pilvia, snow on the coast is rare, falling on average once every 10 years, in the mountainous north, snow is present from November to February.</p>

<h2>Demographics</h2>
<p>A vast majority of the population are native pilvian humans, with human minorities of reani, garmish, kherovan and vihri descent present in many towns. Northern Grevenna is home to a large population of Ogres, that reside in the western Edessa mountains and there is a surprisingly large gnomish population present in southern Grevenna. Few <a href="Elf" class="internal-link">elven</a> and half-elves permanently reside in Grevenna, following the gethric pogroms of the 8th century although they are given full rights in contemporary Grevenna.</p>
<h2>Economy</h2>
<p>Agriculture is still, as basically everywhere in Inara, the main industry within Grevenna, although the countries mining, metallurgy and silk production industries are major elements of its contemporary economy. Owing to its geographic advantages, the port city of Melldock often is the first destination for a variety of luxury goods and spices imported from the south and east, its significant influence on the trade republic of Reanport, amicable agreements with the <a href="Grand Ziflis Company" class="internal-link">G.Z.C.</a>, and good relations with Hainach and Garmeley have made Grevenna into a mercantile profiteer and large scale importer, although the opening of Horad Isthmus have hurt the Lorship on paper. </p>
<h2>Religion</h2>
<small><i>see <a href="Gethricism" class="internal-link">Gethricism</a></i></small>
<p>The country is 94% gethric, following the church of Iztaw and it has been the state religion since the days of the Great Rebellion. <a href="Lyandra" class="internal-link">Lyandra of Fairriver</a>, a major Saint of the Church, is the most popular religious figure within Grevenna, after the prophet himself of course. Her Abbey at Fairriver is a haven for orphans and refugees of wars all over Ilrakhan and a destination for pilgrims, trinicist, kethric and gethric alike.
<br>Spirit worship is officially outlawed but largely tolerated in rural communities, small <a href="Kethricism" class="internal-link">kethric</a> communities in the east of the country are granted rights of practice. </p>
<h2>Culture</h2>
<p>Although religiously and politically closely aligned to its northern neighbour Vihr, modern day Grevenna still embraces traditional reani values like equality and a somewhat open approach to magic. </p>
<h2>Politics</h2>
<p>The country of Grevenna is ruled by the Grand Lord, a rejection of the reani title of Prince and reference to elder days of pilvian independence and age of legends. The Grand Lord is succeeded in accordance to principles of absolute primogeniture by their firstborn child, to this date the current Grand Lord Caroman I is a direct descendant of the first Grand Lord of Grevenna, Antigor I. </p>
<p>It is closely aligned with the <a href="Vihr" class="internal-link">Rascy zi Vihr</a> and has been exacting significant influence over the Republic of <a href="Reanport" class="internal-link">Reanport</a>, still claiming the "Jewel of the Stormy Sea" as rightfully part of its own territory. Relations with eastern <a href="Kherova" class="internal-link">Kherova</a> have fallen in recent years for many reasons, including trade conflicts, border disputes in the yellow moor, and a growing disagreement with practices of the <a href="kherovan Inquisition" class="internal-link">Inquisition</a>. The cursed City of Kulum is still controlled by the Inquisition formally but its land was always claimed as de jure Pilvia and, as everything west of the Opuzna River, owned by the Grand Lord of Grevenna. </p>
<p>The escalating <a href="Garmish Succession Crisis" class="internal-link">Garmish War in the west</a> drawing in more and more countries, including Reanport itself on the one hand and international attitudes towards magic and the Inquisition shifting following the Di-Pora Revolution and the increase in inquisitorial oversight caused by the <a href="Niedrest Incident" class="internal-link">Niedrest Incident of 923PA</a> on the other, have the Lordship torn between two prizes on both sides of its borders. In the mean time many advocate for peace in the wake of unsure relations with the southern Serpent, the mighty <a href="Elbae" class="internal-link">Sultanate</a> can never be given the opportunity to revive the old grudges of the brutal wars of the late 8th century.</p>
<h2>History</h2>
<p><small><i>for a more detailed explanation of the Rebellion and its Lead up see <a href="Antigor I" class="internal-link">Antigor I and the Great War for Independence</a></i></small></p>
<p>Following Antigor I and Elena I,  during the Rule of Grand Lady Elena II, the Witch Bezelia established a domain of dark magic and occult tyranny in northern Grevenna, centred around her domain in the southern Edessa mountains. Her seduction and subjugation of many ogre tribes combined with cruses upon land, animal and man spelled doom for the people of pilvia until the legendary hero and later gethric Saint <a href="Yelena" class="internal-link">Yelena of Dreva</a> slew her in 596PA. There are many tales of Yelenas journey into Edessa and her rivalry with Bezelia but the gethric canon has largely stamped out the more "unorthodox" rumours concerning Yelenas relationship with the Witch. </p>
<p>Grevenna would maintain a strong stance against the Kings of Rean following the century after its victorious war of Independence and actively support the insurgents of the fatal Reanport Uprising of 621PA that would lead to the end of the du Rean royal line and the tragic demise of Yelena.</p>
<p>War would break out with the neighbouring Kathrym of Kherova in 660PA, later known as the Yellow War due to much of the fighting occurring in the Yellow Moor. For decades prior, border disputes in the moor and the southern lands surrounding Kulum had led to skirmishes and discord, eventually leading to all out war. While the armies of Grevenna remained largely dominant on the battlefield, the long siege of Kulum could not garner success and arcane efforts of the surprisingly active magical community within the grevennan army were demolished by Kherovan Spellbreaker regiments. After eight years of gruelling fighting and no end to the Siege of Kulum in sight peace was signed in 668PA at Kulum by Grand Lord Stefan I and Khat Tiblin VII, not to be confused with the <a href="Treaty of Kulum" class="internal-link">Treaty of Kulum</a>. <br>While Kulum was officially acknowledged as a kherovan city the lands of the Opuzna delta and large stretches of the yellow moor were accepted as de jure Grevenni, leading to a strange situation in which Kulum was a kherovan city under kherovan governance on foreign territory. This solution would incite conflict 200 years later when Kulum fell under the reign of <a href="Ahrmiz Saceren" class="internal-link">Ahrmiz Saceren</a>.</p>

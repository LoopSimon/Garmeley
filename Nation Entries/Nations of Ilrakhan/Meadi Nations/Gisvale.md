<p>It is impossible to tell the history of Meadrath without also telling the history of Gisvale, the rich farmlands around the bends of the Trinmo have seen great historic wars, Kings and Emperors and multiple civil wars, but always Giselstatt remained one of the most important cities on the subcontinent. Now the "Gate to Meadland" is ruled as an Archduchy under <a href="Meadrath" class="internal-link">Meadrath</a> and afforded a great amount of freedom, much to the dismay of its northern rival <a href="Lesmere" class="internal-link">Lesmere</a>.</p>
<p>Gisvale stretches from the waterfall at Erlmeet where the Erl flows into the Trinmo, all the way along the Bend of the River to Trinfurt and the Silentree Forest. The lands of the actual Gisvale, south of Giselstatt and north of the Kortopi mountains are luscious and arable farmlands that have historically supported a larger population that its northern neighbour Lesmere. The feet of Kortopi hills used to be ruled by one of the fearsome Ogrelords of the past and as such the higher elevation hills and vales are still pestered by the brutish creatures. The border disputes with Lesmere mainly concern the Urien plains to the east on the other side of the Trinmos Bend, although both Duchies tread careful to avoid escalaction and confrontation with the City of <a href="Uhl" class="internal-link">Uhl</a> itself.</p>

<h3>Lead Up</h3>
<p>The Marival of Tusul saw itself in an increasingly weaker economic position during the 7th and 8th centuries domination of the Beheran Coast by the elves of the Sultanate, whereas the largely isolationist realm entertained good relations with the Marival of Yorgos and the Safiri Empire of Pravazik to their south and west, the Sultanates policies of early imperialism and diplomatic encroachment threatened Tusuli independence more and more. Tusul had always dependent somewhat on its neighbours, being a landlocked nation whos capabilities for large scale agriculture and efficient farming were severly limited by its mountainous lands and the merciless nature of the beheran desert.  </p>

<p>The mid to late 8th and early 9th century saw the Sultanate go through large scale military campaigns during the three <a href="Wars of Elbae Aggression" class="internal-link">Wars against the Reani Coalition</a>, Sultan Arbalas I was seen as the figurehead for elven military strength and its dominance over the Deserts of Behera, even when faced with a coalition of many powerful realms in the north the Sultante had prevailed. With the end of these Wars and the period of peace and recovery following the year 815PA, Arbalas health would begin to rapidly decline. When in the year 844PA, the Sultan of the Elves passed on, the traditional elven year of mourning had already begun decades prior: The Succession of Arbalas was a hard-fought and contested issues we shall not go into details about here, but it resulted in Irater, third of his name, Arbalas grandson from his second born child, to assume power and sit the White Chair of Jiravid.</p>
<p>Irater was internationally considered an unstable and weak-willed ruler, the fear of a man like this wanting to prove himself before his ancestors at the helm of a powerful Empire like the Sultanate was clear: With war in the north proving risky and uncertain, the remaining independent human Marivali of the beheran humans would make for an excellent target.</p>
<p>The richer and more populous Marival of Yorgos, Tusuls old neighbour, would agree to support the Marival tentatively, in the case of an expansionist war but decline to begin hostile action themselves or the like. Its trade within the greater Elbae Sphere of Prosperity dependant heavily on friendly relations with the elves, and its mercantile route through the desert connecting the Kaitan Gulf with the Empire of Pravazik was a goldmine of elven commerce.</p>
<h3>The assassination attempt on Irater III</h3><p>The exact details, the true perpetrators and their motivations and the plans that would pre amble this even are unknown, multiple theories from all sides, Tusuli, Yorgosi and Elven exist that in large parts contradict each other.</p>
<p>The fact is that during the Divine Procession of the first days year 848PA, Sultan Irater III was almost murdered in open daylight on his way to the Grand Tomb of Paret in Biadarkyr. This magical assault included the destruction of large parts of the Temple road, the murder of the then Grand Hierophant of the Golden Dusk, Ud Ze-harrad, the sacrifice of many of Iraters personal guard including his eldest Son, Jirbalas and the death of almost 300 onlookers.</p>
<p>Shouts of Zipivi warcries and Tusuli patriotism were heard, and 11 of the assailants would later on be caught alive and profess under torture to have acted on behalf of the Marival of Tusul, to prevent war.</p>
<p>While the elven standpoint has always been that such a cowardly attack was indeed the decided action of the Tusuli Marival and effectively a declaration of war in itself, many historians and political spectators have questioned the validity of these testimonies. Not only being produced under torture but also considering the open display of pro Tusul sentiment during the attack itself as it would surely always have led to war, Iraters death or survival being irrelevant. Yorgos distanced itself immediately from the attacks and disavowed the actions of these Tusuli assassins ending their military alliance and guarantee of defensive protection with their northern neighbour. Within 5 months of the attack, the southern armies of the Sultanate had mobilized south of Biadarkyr and the decade long White Wars began. </p>
